# Competency Framework Design

## Purpose
Design comprehensive competency frameworks including skill taxonomies, proficiency levels, behavioral indicators, and competency models for effective talent management.

## Template

```
You are a competency framework designer and talent development specialist. Create a competency framework for:

Organization Context:
- Organization: [ORGANIZATION_NAME]
- Role/Level: [TARGET_ROLE_LEVEL]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Framework Purpose: [PURPOSE]

Generate comprehensive competency framework:

## COMPETENCY ARCHITECTURE

### Core Competencies
- Universal skills applicable across all roles
- Organizational values and culture
- Communication and collaboration
- Problem-solving and critical thinking
- Adaptability and learning agility
- Ethics and integrity

### Technical Competencies
- Role-specific technical skills
- Domain knowledge and expertise
- Tools and technology proficiency
- Methods and best practices
- Industry-specific knowledge
- Certifications and credentials

### Leadership Competencies
- Strategic thinking and vision
- People management and development
- Change management and influence
- Decision-making and judgment
- Innovation and entrepreneurship
- Stakeholder management

### Behavioral Competencies
- Emotional intelligence
- Teamwork and collaboration
- Initiative and drive
- Customer focus
- Quality orientation
- Resilience and stress management

## PROFICIENCY FRAMEWORK

### Proficiency Levels
**Level 1: Awareness**
- Basic understanding
- Requires supervision
- Learning phase

**Level 2: Working Knowledge**
- Can perform with guidance
- Developing proficiency
- Occasional supervision

**Level 3: Proficient**
- Independent performance
- Consistent quality
- Can guide others

**Level 4: Advanced**
- Expert performance
- Mentors and trains others
- Process improvement

**Level 5: Expert/Master**
- Thought leader
- Creates standards
- Industry recognition

### Behavioral Indicators
- Observable behaviors for each level
- Performance examples
- Evidence requirements
- Assessment criteria

## COMPETENCY MODELS

### Role-Based Models
- Job family competencies
- Level-specific requirements
- Career progression pathways
- Transition requirements

### Functional Models
- Department-specific competencies
- Cross-functional requirements
- Shared competencies
- Specialized skills

## DELIVERABLES

- Competency framework document
- Skill taxonomy and definitions
- Proficiency level descriptions
- Behavioral indicator library
- Role competency models
- Assessment criteria

Ensure framework is clear, measurable, and aligned to organizational needs.
```

## Usage Example
Use for building competency frameworks, defining skill requirements, or creating development pathways.
