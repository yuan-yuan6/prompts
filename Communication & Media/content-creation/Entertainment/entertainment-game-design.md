# Game Design Readiness Assessment

## Purpose
Comprehensively assess a game design's readiness for production across six dimensions: Core Design Vision, Narrative & World-Building, Mechanics & Systems, Level & Environment Design, User Experience & Interface, and Production & Market. This framework identifies design gaps, validates player experience goals, and ensures production-ready documentation before development begins.

- 1.0-1.9: Concept Stage (high-level ideas, major gaps, not production-ready)
- 2.0-2.9: Design Development (foundational elements defined, significant refinement needed)

- 4.0-4.9: Polished Design (thorough documentation, optimization focus, best practices)
- 5.0: Industry-Leading (innovative, comprehensive, reference-quality documentation)

### Example 1: Indie Studio â€” First Commercial Game Design

**Context:** A three-person indie team (designer, programmer, artist) is assessing their first commercial game design â€” a 2D roguelike platformer titled "Chrono Chambers" targeting PC via Steam. They have a prototype demonstrating core movement and combat but need to validate design completeness before seeking publisher funding.

**Assessment Highlights:**
- **Current Overall Score: 2.6/5.0** (Design Development stage)
- **Core Design Vision: 3.2/5.0** â€” Strong foundation with clear gameplay loop (time-manipulation platforming + combat), compelling player fantasy (mastering temporal powers), and defensible USP (reversible death mechanic where dying rewinds time but leaves "ghost" obstacles). Emotional goals align well with roguelike mastery fantasy. Weakness: visual style direction inconsistent between concept art pieces, comparable game analysis too narrow (only examined Celeste and Hades, missing relevant time-manipulation games like Braid or Superhot).

- **Narrative & World-Building: 1.9/5.0** â€” Minimal development. High-concept premise exists (time-manipulating thief infiltrating a reality-shifting tower) but lacks protagonist motivation depth, supporting character plans, environmental storytelling strategy, or lore integration into gameplay. Story delivery mechanism undefined â€” team debating between minimalist approach (like Downwell) vs narrative focus (like Hades). Critical gap blocking art direction decisions.

- **Mechanics & Systems: 3.8/5.0** â€” Strongest dimension. Core time-rewind mechanic demonstrably fun in prototype playtesting. Three primary abilities (Rewind, Slow-Motion, Future-Echo) interact synergistically creating emergent puzzle solutions. Progression system well-designed with meta-progression (permanent upgrades) and run-based progression (temporary power-ups) balancing short-term and long-term goals. Secondary mechanics (shop system, NPC interactions) appropriately support core loop without distraction. Minor weakness: late-game difficulty curve potentially too steep based on designer playthroughs (not yet validated with external players at appropriate skill levels).

- **Level & Environment Design: 2.4/5.0** â€” Philosophy clear (procedurally-generated rooms with hand-crafted challenge templates), but only 8 room templates exist vs 50+ needed for launch variety. Pacing strategy well-conceived (alternating combat/puzzle rooms, shops every 5 rooms, bosses every 15 rooms) but not tested with full content. Environmental storytelling strategy undefined. Replayability factors strong due to roguelike structure, but daily challenge mode and seeded runs not yet designed.

- **User Experience & Interface: 2.9/5.0** â€” UI philosophy established (minimalist spatial UI showing time-manipulation charges near player character, diegetic health bar as visual character damage). HUD design rough but functional. Menu systems incomplete (no settings menu, pause lacks proper options, death screen basic). Critical gap: no accessibility features planned despite fast-paced gameplay potentially challenging for players with reaction time limitations. Controller support designed but not implemented.

- **Production & Market Readiness: 2.1/5.0** â€” Technical feasibility high (Unity 2D project running smoothly, all mechanics prototyped). Team capability gap: no experience shipping commercial games, marketing knowledge minimal, business development unfamiliar. Timeline overly optimistic (6-month estimate should be 12-15 months based on content scope). Monetization model defined (premium $19.99 purchase with post-launch cosmetic DLC). Competitive analysis insufficient (missing recent roguelike releases, pricing strategy not validated against comparable indie titles). No community-building strategy or wishlist-building plan despite being 6+ months from launch.

**Critical Gaps Identified:**
1. **Narrative Direction Undefined** (Impact: High, Urgency: Critical) â€” Story ambiguity blocking art direction, environmental design, and UI tone decisions. Team split between minimalist and narrative-rich approaches. **Action Required:** 2-week narrative sprint to decide approach, create 1-page story summary, define 3 core characters, establish environmental storytelling pillars. If narrative-rich chosen, requires hiring contract writer or significantly extending timeline. Decision blocks 30% of asset production pipeline.

2. **Content Production Underestimated** (Impact: Critical, Urgency: High) â€” Only 8 room templates exist vs 50+ needed. At current 3-days-per-room pace, requires 126 additional days (6 months of solo artist work), not accounting for enemy variety, boss designs, environmental art sets, VFX, and UI assets. **Action Required:** Implement procedural variation systems to multiply template value, simplify art style to accelerate production (currently detailed pixel art), or extend timeline to 15+ months. Consider contracting additional pixel artist for 3-month sprint.

3. **External Playtesting Absent** (Impact: High, Urgency: Medium) â€” All difficulty tuning and fun validation based solely on designer's 200+ hours playing own game, creating expertise bias. No data on onboarding clarity, difficulty curve appropriateness, or core mechanic fun for fresh players. **Action Required:** Recruit 10-15 playtesters across skill levels for structured playtesting sessions. Implement telemetry system tracking death locations, ability usage frequency, room completion times, and run length distribution. Schedule bi-weekly playtests for remaining development.

4. **Monetization & Marketing Strategy Immature** (Impact: High, Urgency: Medium) â€” $19.99 pricing not validated against market data (recent successful indie roguelikes range from $14.99-$24.99), no wishlist-building strategy despite being critical for Steam algorithm visibility, zero social media presence or community building, and post-launch DLC plans vague. **Action Required:** Competitive pricing analysis of 20 comparable titles released 2023-2025, establish Steam page 6 months before launch to build wishlists, create dev blog documenting progress for community engagement, define 3 specific DLC concepts with development cost estimates.

5. **Accessibility Features Absent** (Impact: Medium, Urgency: Medium) â€” Fast-paced time-manipulation gameplay potentially excludes players with slower reaction times, no assist mode or difficulty modifiers planned, colorblind considerations not addressed (time powers use color coding: blue=rewind, red=slowmo, green=future-echo). **Action Required:** Design assist mode with gameplay speed adjustment (50%-150% speed), invincibility toggle, ability cooldown modifiers, and damage multipliers. Implement colorblind modes and add icon shapes to color-coded abilities. Consider these accessibility features as potential differentiators in marketing.

**Recommended Action Plan** (12-Month Timeline to Production-Ready):

*Months 1-2: Design Completion Sprint*
- Week 1-2: Narrative direction decision, 1-page story doc, environmental storytelling pillars
- Week 3-4: Content production system overhaul (procedural variations, art simplification decision)
- Week 5-6: Accessibility features design, assist mode specification
- Week 7-8: External playtest recruiting, telemetry system implementation, first playtest sessions

*Months 3-4: Content Production Ramp-Up*
- Expand room templates from 8 to 30 (minimum viable variety)
- Create 5 boss encounter designs
- Implement procedural variation systems
- Bi-weekly playtesting with difficulty tuning

*Months 5-6: Polish & Secondary Systems*
- Complete UI/menu systems (settings, pause, accessibility options)
- Implement controller support
- Add meta-progression content (permanent upgrades, unlocks)
- Continue playtesting and balance iteration

*Months 7-9: Vertical Slice & Publisher Pitch*
- Complete 30-minute polished vertical slice (first 3 biomes fully playable)
- Create pitch deck and gameplay trailer
- Establish Steam page and begin wishlist building
- Pitch to 10+ indie publishers for potential funding/marketing support

*Months 10-12: Full Production (if funded) or Continued Development (if bootstrapping)*
- Expand to full 50+ room templates, all bosses, complete progression systems
- Ongoing playtesting and polish
- Community building via dev blog, social media, demo releases
- Prepare for Early Access or full launch

**Success Metrics:**
- Core Design Vision: 3.2 â†’ 4.0 (refine visual style consistency, expand comparable game analysis)
- Narrative & World-Building: 1.9 â†’ 3.0 minimum (decide approach, create essential story docs)
- Mechanics & Systems: 3.8 â†’ 4.2 (validate difficulty curve with external players, fine-tune balance)
- Level & Environment Design: 2.4 â†’ 3.5 (expand to 50+ templates, implement procedural variations)
- User Experience & Interface: 2.9 â†’ 3.8 (complete menu systems, add accessibility features, implement controller support)
- Production & Market Readiness: 2.1 â†’ 3.5 (realistic timeline, validated pricing, community building started, publisher relationships established)
- **Target Overall: 2.6 â†’ 3.5+** (production-ready for full development commitment)

### Example 2: AA Studio â€” Live Service Game Expansion Assessment

**Assessment Highlights:**

- **Mechanics & Systems: 3.8/5.0** â€” Cold survival mechanics thoroughly designed (temperature management, hypothermia stages, cold resistance gear, warming mechanics) with clear integration into existing survival systems without invalidating base game strategies. - **Level & Environment Design: 3.2/5.0** â€” Snow biome spatial design philosophy consistent with base game's "exploration-rewarding open world" approach, size appropriately scoped (3 zones matching base game's biome scale), pacing strategy alternates combat outposts with story locations, and environmental storytelling embeds lore through abandoned research stations. **Critical Gaps Identified:**
