## Usage Examples

### Example 1: Acute Chest Pain Evaluation
**Context**: 55-year-old male presenting with acute onset chest pain

**Implementation**:
- Presentation: Substernal pressure, radiating to left arm, associated with diaphoresis and dyspnea
- Differentials: Acute coronary syndrome, pulmonary embolism, aortic dissection, pneumothorax
- Workup: ECG, troponins, D-dimer, chest X-ray, possible CT angiography
- Clinical reasoning: High pre-test probability for ACS given risk factors and presentation
- Management: Aspirin, nitroglycerin, morphine, cardiology consultation, cath lab activation if STEMI

### Example 2: Chronic Fatigue Investigation
**Context**: 35-year-old female with 6-month history of progressive fatigue

**Implementation**:
- Presentation: Persistent fatigue, unrefreshing sleep, post-exertional malaise, cognitive difficulties
- Differentials: Chronic fatigue syndrome, thyroid disease, anemia, depression, autoimmune conditions
- Workup: CBC, CMP, TSH, B12, folate, ferritin, ANA, ESR, CRP, cortisol, sleep study consideration
- Clinical reasoning: Systematic exclusion of organic causes before considering functional diagnosis
- Follow-up: Serial assessments, symptom diary, activity pacing, multidisciplinary approach

### Example 3: Pediatric Fever Without Source
**Context**: 18-month-old with high fever and no localizing symptoms

**Implementation**:
- Presentation: Fever 39.5Â°C, irritability, decreased oral intake, no specific symptoms
- Differentials: Occult bacteremia, UTI, viral syndrome, early meningitis, Kawasaki disease
- Workup: Urinalysis, urine culture, CBC with differential, blood culture, consider LP based on age
- Clinical reasoning: Age-based risk stratification, Rochester/Philadelphia/Boston criteria application
- Management: Empiric antibiotics if high risk, close observation if low risk, parent education

## Customization Options

### Option 1: Emergency Medicine Focus
Prioritize rapid triage, life-threatening exclusions, time-sensitive diagnoses, emergency interventions, and disposition planning with minute-by-minute decision making.

### Option 2: Primary Care Approach
Emphasize comprehensive evaluation, preventive care integration, chronic disease management, continuity of care, and cost-effective workup with longitudinal perspective.

### Option 3: Specialty Consultation Mode
Focus on complex referrals, tertiary care evaluation, rare disease consideration, advanced diagnostics, and expert opinion with detailed subspecialty assessment.

### Option 4: Telemedicine Adaptation
Optimize for remote assessment, limited physical exam, home monitoring, digital health tools, and follow-up planning with technology-enabled care delivery.

### Option 5: Resource-Limited Setting
Adapt for basic diagnostics, clinical diagnosis emphasis, empiric treatment, essential investigations only, and public health considerations with cost-conscious approach.
