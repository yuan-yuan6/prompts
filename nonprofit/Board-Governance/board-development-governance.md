# Nonprofit Board Development & Governance

## Purpose
Comprehensive framework for building effective nonprofit boards including recruitment, onboarding, governance structures, fiduciary duties, board engagement, and ensuring legal compliance and organizational effectiveness.

## Quick Start

**Need to strengthen your board?** Use this minimal example:

### Minimal Example
```
Develop board development plan for education nonprofit including board composition analysis, recruitment of diverse skilled members, onboarding program, committee structure, meeting effectiveness, fiduciary training, and annual board evaluation.
```

### When to Use This
- Building a new nonprofit board
- Strengthening existing board effectiveness
- Board recruitment and succession planning
- Improving board governance practices
- Meeting legal and compliance requirements

### Basic 3-Step Workflow
1. **Assess & Plan** - Board composition, gaps, needs (2-4 weeks)
2. **Recruit & Onboard** - Identify, recruit, orient new members (2-3 months)
3. **Engage & Develop** - Training, effectiveness, evaluation (ongoing)

**Time to complete**: Ongoing board development, initial transformation 6-12 months

## BOARD GOVERNANCE FRAMEWORK

### 1. Board Composition & Structure
**Optimal Board Size:**
- Small nonprofit (budget <$1M): 5-12 members
- Medium nonprofit ($1M-$10M): 12-20 members
- Large nonprofit (>$10M): 15-30 members
- Balance: engaged vs manageable

**Diversity & Skills Mix:**
- Professional expertise (legal, finance, marketing, fundraising)
- Industry knowledge (mission-related experience)
- Demographic diversity (race, gender, age, geography)
- Constituent representation
- Community connections and networks
- Fundraising capacity

**Board Officers:**
- Board Chair/President
- Vice Chair
- Secretary
- Treasurer
- Committee Chairs

**Terms & Succession:**
- Typical: 3-year terms, two consecutive terms
- Staggered terms for continuity
- Term limits to ensure fresh perspectives
- Succession planning for leadership
- Alumni engagement

Provide:
- Board composition matrix
- Skills assessment tool
- Term structure recommendations
- Succession plan template

### 2. Board Recruitment
**Recruitment Planning:**
- Annual board development plan
- Identify skill and diversity gaps
- Create ideal board member profile
- Prospecting sources
- Nominating committee leadership

**Candidate Identification:**
- Current board referrals
- Community leader nominations
- Professional networks
- Client and donor bases
- Volunteer pool
- Industry associations

**Recruitment Process:**
- Initial outreach and exploration
- Organizational overview presentation
- Expectations and responsibilities clarification
- One-on-one meetings with board leaders
- Board meeting observation
- Formal nomination and election

**Expectations Setting:**
- Time commitment (meetings, events, work)
- Fundraising expectations (give and get)
- Committee participation
- Advocacy and ambassadorship
- Term of service
- Conflict of interest disclosure

Provide:

### 4. Fiduciary Duties & Legal Responsibilities
**Duty of Care:**
- Attend meetings and come prepared
- Ask questions and seek information
- Exercise independent judgment
- Hire and oversee executive director
- Review financial statements
- Approve budgets and major decisions

**Duty of Loyalty:**
- Act in organization's best interest
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Disclose conflicts and recuse
- Protect confidential information
- No self-dealing or private benefit
- Whistleblower protections

**Duty of Obedience:**
- Ensure mission adherence
- Comply with laws and regulations
- Follow bylaws and policies
- Maintain tax-exempt status
- File required reports (Form 990)
- Protect charitable assets

**Legal Compliance:**
- State incorporation and registration
- Federal tax exemption (501c3)
- Annual Form 990 filing
- State charitable solicitation registration
- Employment laws and worker classification
- Insurance coverage (D&O, liability)

Provide:
- Fiduciary duties training
- Legal compliance checklist
- Conflict of interest policy
- Whistleblower policy
- Insurance review

### 5. Board Meetings & Engagement
**Effective Meetings:**
- Annual meeting calendar
- Advance agenda and materials (1 week prior)
- Focused agenda and time management
- Consent agenda for routine items
- Strategic discussions vs reports
- Action items and follow-up
- Minutes and documentation

**Committee Structure:**
- Executive Committee
- Finance/Audit Committee
- Governance/Nominating Committee
- Fundraising/Development Committee
- Program Committee
- Advocacy/Public Policy Committee
- Mission-specific committees

**Committee Effectiveness:**
- Clear charges and work plans
- Regular meetings and reporting
- Staff support and liaison
- Non-board committee members
- Time-bound task forces
- Sunset non-essential committees

**Board Engagement:**
- Annual board retreat
- Program site visits
- Client interactions
- Fundraising events
- Advocacy activities
- Skills-based volunteering
- Social and team building

Provide:

- Agenda templates

### 6. Board-Staff Partnership
**Board-Executive Director Relationship:**
- Clear role delineation
- Board governs, staff manages
- Regular communication
- Annual ED performance review
- Succession planning
- Support and accountability

**Role Clarity:**
- Board: mission, strategy, resources, oversight
- Staff: implementation, operations, management
- Avoid micromanagement
- Respect expertise
- Partnership mindset

**Communication:**
- Regular board-ED check-ins
- Transparent and timely information
- Bad news travels up quickly
- Celebrate successes
- Constructive feedback

Provide:

## Best Practices

- **Mission First** - All decisions serve the mission
- **Clear Expectations** - Define roles and responsibilities upfront
- **Diversity Matters** - Build inclusive, representative boards
- **Engage Actively** - Board service requires meaningful involvement
- **Strategic Focus** - Govern, don't manage
- **Continuous Learning** - Invest in board development
- **Evaluate Regularly** - Assess and improve effectiveness
- **Partnership Mindset** - Board and staff work together
