## GRADING SYSTEMS

### Traditional Letter Grades
A (90-100%): Excellent/Outstanding
B (80-89%): Good/Above Average
C (70-79%): Satisfactory/Average
D (60-69%): Below Average/Passing
F (Below 60%): Failing/Unsatisfactory

### Standards-Based Grading
4 - Exceeds Standard
3 - Meets Standard
2 - Approaching Standard
1 - Below Standard
N/A - Not Yet Assessed

### Pass/Fail Grading
P - Pass (meets minimum standard)
F - Fail (below minimum standard)
I - Incomplete (work not submitted)

### Specifications Grading
✓+ Excellent
✓ Satisfactory
✓- Needs Revision
✗ Not Acceptable

## Best Practices

1. **Communicate expectations early** - Share grading criteria at course start
2. **Be consistent** - Apply standards equally to all students
3. **Provide timely feedback** - Return work within one week when possible
4. **Focus on learning** - Grades should reflect achievement, not compliance
5. **Offer opportunities** - Allow revisions and multiple attempts when appropriate
6. **Use rubrics** - Provide clear, objective scoring criteria
7. **Track progress** - Maintain accurate and complete records
8. **Protect privacy** - Keep grades confidential
9. **Be available** - Offer office hours for questions
10. **Reflect and improve** - Regularly evaluate grading practices

## Tips for Success

- Use learning management systems to streamline grading
- Create comment banks for common feedback
- Schedule dedicated grading time
- Grade one assignment at a time for consistency
- Use rubrics to speed up grading and improve feedback
- Involve students in self and peer assessment
- Communicate grade meanings clearly to all stakeholders
- Provide exemplars of different performance levels
- Offer multiple opportunities to demonstrate learning
- Focus feedback on most important improvements
