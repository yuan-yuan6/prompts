# Market Entry Strategy Framework

## Purpose
Systematically evaluate market attractiveness, determine optimal entry mode, and develop comprehensive go-to-market strategy for entering new geographic markets, product markets, or customer segments while managing execution risks.

## Quick Start

**Want to evaluate a market entry opportunity quickly?** Here's the minimal approach:

### When to Use This
- Considering expansion into new geographic markets (domestic or international)
- Evaluating entry into adjacent product/service markets
- Assessing new customer segment opportunities
- Planning acquisition of market entry through M&A
- Responding to competitive threats in new markets

### Minimal Example
```
Company: B2B SaaS Platform (Current: North America)
Target Market: European Union (Germany, France, UK)
Quick Assessment:
- Market Size: €2.5B TAM, growing 15% annually ✅
- Competitive Intensity: 3 local players, 2 US competitors already entered ⚠️
- Regulatory: GDPR compliance required, manageable ✅
- Customer Fit: Similar enterprise needs, some localization needed ⚠️
- Entry Mode Recommendation: Partnership with local reseller → Own sales team after year 1
- Investment Required: €1.5M Year 1, breakeven by Year 3
Decision: PROCEED with phased approach, Germany first
```

### Basic 5-Step Workflow
1. **Assess Market Attractiveness** - Size, growth, profitability potential
2. **Analyze Competitive Landscape** - Who's there, how strong, barriers to overcome
3. **Evaluate Entry Modes** - Export, license, franchise, JV, acquisition, greenfield
4. **Select Entry Strategy** - Choose mode and develop detailed plan
5. **Plan Execution & Risk Mitigation** - Timeline, resources, contingencies

**Time to complete**: 1-2 weeks for initial assessment, 4-8 weeks for comprehensive strategy

**Pro tip**: Start with one beachhead market segment or geography - don't try to enter entire market at once

## PHASE 2: ENTRY MODE EVALUATION

Evaluate each entry mode against criteria:

### 2.1 Exporting (Direct or Indirect)

**Description:** Produce in home market, sell in target market

**Pros:**
- Low investment and risk
- Leverage existing production capacity
- Quick market entry
- Easy to exit

**Cons:**
- High transportation costs
- Trade barriers (tariffs)
- Limited market knowledge
- Less control over customer experience

**Fit Assessment:** [HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW]
**Conditions favoring export:** [WHEN TO USE]

### 2.2 Licensing/Franchising

**Description:** Grant rights to local partner to use IP/brand/business model

**Pros:**
- No capital investment
- Partner provides local knowledge
- Rapid expansion possible
- Low political risk

**Cons:**
- Limited control over operations
- Risk of creating future competitor
- Dependent on partner performance
- Lower profit margins

**Fit Assessment:** [HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW]
**Conditions favoring licensing:** [WHEN TO USE]

### 2.3 Strategic Alliance/Joint Venture

**Description:** Partner with local company to jointly enter market

**Pros:**
- Share risks and costs
- Access partner's local knowledge and networks
- Combine complementary strengths
- May be required by regulation

**Cons:**
- Partner conflict potential
- Shared control and profits
- Complex governance
- Exit challenges

## Usage Examples

### Example 1: Starbucks Entry into China
**Context:** US coffee chain entering tea-drinking market

**Key Decisions:**
- **Entry Mode**: Joint venture → Wholly-owned (acquired partner stakes over time)
- **Localization**: Tea-based drinks, store as "third place" for business meetings
- **Beachhead**: Shanghai and Beijing first, tier 1 cities
- **Pricing**: Premium positioning (affordability + aspiration)

**Result:** Became #1 coffee chain in China with 6,000+ stores

---

### Example 2: Netflix International Expansion
**Context:** Streaming service global expansion

**Key Decisions:**
- **Entry Mode**: Greenfield (wholly-owned subsidiaries)
- **Sequence**: English-speaking markets first → Europe → Latin America → Asia
- **Localization**: Local content commissioning, subtitles/dubbing, payment methods
- **Strategy**: Technology-led, rapid multi-market launches

**Result:** 60%+ of subscribers now outside US

---

### Example 3: IKEA Entry into India
**Context:** Swedish furniture retailer entering price-sensitive market

**Key Decisions:**
- **Entry Mode**: Greenfield after regulation allowed 100% FDI in retail
- **Localization**: Smaller apartments → Smaller furniture, lower price points
- **Beachhead**: Hyderabad (growing middle class, less competition than Mumbai/Delhi)
- **Supply Chain**: 50% local sourcing to reduce costs

**Result:** Successful launch, plans for 25 stores by 2025

## Best Practices

### Do's ✅
- **Start Small**: Beachhead approach reduces risk
- **Test Assumptions**: Pilot before full commitment
- **Invest in Local Knowledge**: Hire locally, use local partners
- **Adapt Thoughtfully**: Balance standardization with localization
- **Plan for Long Term**: Market entry often takes longer than expected
- **Build Flexibility**: Maintain ability to pivot or exit
- **Learn Continuously**: Treat initial phase as learning period

### Don'ts ❌
- **Don't Underestimate Differences**: Cultural and regulatory differences are real
- **Don't Skip Due Diligence**: Inadequate research causes failures
- **Don't Over-Commit Too Fast**: Phased approach allows course correction
- **Don't Ignore Local Competition**: They have home advantage
- **Don't Assume Success Transfers**: What works in home market may not travel
- **Don't Neglect Exit Planning**: Need graceful exit if strategy fails
