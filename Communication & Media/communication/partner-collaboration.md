# Partner Collaboration & Strategic Alliances Readiness Assessment

## Purpose
Assess readiness to build **successful strategic partnerships** that drive mutual value, innovation, and market expansion. Use this to diagnose gaps in partner strategy, enablement, governance, and performance management.

## Template

Conduct a partner collaboration & strategic alliances readiness assessment for {PARTNER_CONTEXT}.

Score each dimension **1â€“5** (1 = ad hoc, 5 = optimized). Ground findings in observable signals (partner satisfaction, revenue/pipeline, engagement quality, issue resolution).

**1) PARTNER STRATEGY & VALUE PROPOSITION**
- Partner types and tiers are defined (strategic, channel, tech, referral)
- Mutual value proposition is clear and validated with partners
- Ideal partner profile and selection criteria exist
- Portfolio is balanced for strategic fit, market coverage, and risk

**2) ONBOARDING & ENABLEMENT**
- Onboarding plans set clear expectations, milestones, and success criteria
- Training covers product, positioning, sales process, and support
- Resources are accessible (portal, docs, tools, contacts)
- Time-to-first-deal and partner ramp metrics are tracked

**3) GOVERNANCE & COMMUNICATION**
- Governance structure defines roles, decision rights, and escalation paths
- Regular cadence exists (QBRs, ops syncs, executive reviews)
- Communication is proactive and transparent (roadmaps, changes, wins)
- Conflict resolution process is documented and followed

**4) GO-TO-MARKET & CO-SELLING**
- Joint go-to-market plan exists with target segments and plays
- Marketing and sales assets are co-developed and co-branded
- Lead routing, opportunity registration, and deal collaboration are clear
- Partner-sourced pipeline and revenue are tracked and attributed

**5) PERFORMANCE MEASUREMENT & OPTIMIZATION**
- KPIs are defined per partner tier (pipeline, revenue, satisfaction, engagement)
- Scorecards track activity, outcomes, and partnership health
- Performance reviews happen on schedule with action plans
- Partner feedback drives program iteration and improvement

**6) RELATIONSHIP DEPTH & TRUST**
- Executive sponsorship exists for strategic partnerships
- Relationship extends beyond single point of contact
- Partners feel valued and see mutual investment
- Innovation and co-creation opportunities are explored jointly

## Best Practices (Exactly 8)
1) Segment partners by strategic value and tailor enablement, engagement, and resources accordingly.
2) Validate mutual value proposition early; ensure both sides see clear ROI.
3) Onboard with clear milestones and track time-to-first-deal as a leading indicator.
4) Formalize governance with regular QBRs for strategic partners and operational syncs for others.
5) Co-develop GTM assets and plays; avoid one-sided marketing that partners won't use.
6) Measure partner engagement (training completion, portal usage, deal reg) not just revenue.
7) Build exec-to-exec relationships in strategic partnerships; don't stay single-threaded.
8) Prune dormant partners annually; focus resources on those driving mutual value.
