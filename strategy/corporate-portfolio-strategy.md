# Corporate Portfolio Strategy Framework

## Purpose
Systematically evaluate, manage, and optimize portfolio of business units, products, or investments to maximize corporate value through strategic decisions about where to invest, grow, maintain, or divest based on market attractiveness and competitive position.

## Quick Start

**Want to assess your portfolio quickly?** Here's the minimal approach:

### When to Use This
- Managing multiple business units or product lines
- Deciding capital allocation across portfolio
- Evaluating acquisition or divestiture opportunities
- Underperforming businesses draining resources
- Need to focus corporate strategy and resources

### Minimal Example
```
Company: Diversified Industrial Conglomerate
Portfolio: 7 business units across different industries

Quick BCG Matrix Assessment:

Stars (High growth, High share):
- Advanced Materials ($200M, 35% share, 20% growth) → INVEST for leadership

Cash Cows (Low growth, High share):
- Traditional Manufacturing ($500M, 45% share, 3% growth) → HARVEST cash for Stars

Question Marks (High growth, Low share):
- IoT Solutions ($50M, 8% share, 35% growth) → INVEST or DIVEST decision needed
- Green Energy ($30M, 5% share, 40% growth) → DIVEST (can't win)

Dogs (Low growth, Low share):
- Legacy Equipment ($100M, 12% share, -2% growth) → DIVEST
- Regional Services ($75M, 10% share, 1% growth) → DIVEST

Strategy:
- Invest: Advanced Materials (double down), IoT Solutions (targeted investment)
- Harvest: Traditional Manufacturing (milk for cash)
- Divest: Green Energy, Legacy Equipment, Regional Services
- Deploy proceeds from divestitures to Stars and selective Question Marks

Result: Focused portfolio, better capital allocation, 35% increase in ROIC
```

### Basic 5-Step Workflow
1. **Map Current Portfolio** - Inventory all businesses/products
2. **Assess Each Unit** - Market attractiveness and competitive position
3. **Classify & Prioritize** - Use portfolio matrix to categorize
4. **Develop Portfolio Strategy** - Invest/harvest/divest decisions
5. **Execute & Monitor** - Implement changes and track performance

**Time to complete**: 2-3 weeks for comprehensive portfolio review

**Pro tip**: Be ruthless about divesting underperformers - they consume management attention and capital better deployed elsewhere

## Best Practices

### Do's ✅
- **Be Objective**: Use data, not emotional attachment
- **Think Portfolio**: Optimize whole, not individual parts
- **Act Decisively**: Don't let underperformers linger
- **Allocate to Winners**: Put resources where returns are highest
- **Measure Rigorously**: Track actual vs. expected returns
- **Capture Synergies**: Realize the portfolio value
- **Review Regularly**: Markets change, reassess annually

### Don'ts ❌
- **Don't Hoard Losers**: Sunk cost fallacy destroys value
- **Don't Spread Resources Thin**: Focus beats diversification
- **Don't Ignore Fit**: Keep only businesses you can parent well
- **Don't Assume Synergies**: Most are harder than expected
- **Don't Neglect Culture**: Portfolio moves affect people
- **Don't Rush Integration**: Move deliberately on synergies
