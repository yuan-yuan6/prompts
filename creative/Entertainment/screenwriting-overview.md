## 1. STORY CONCEPT FUNDAMENTALS

### High-Concept Development
#### Concept Foundation
- Unique story concept and fresh perspective
- High-concept hook and marketable premise
- Genre-bending and innovative approach
- Universal theme and human truth integration
- Contemporary relevance and cultural resonance
- Commercial viability and audience appeal
- Intellectual property and originality verification
- Franchise potential and expandability consideration

#### Logline Crafting
- Protagonist identification and character definition
- Central conflict and driving force articulation
- Stakes establishment and consequence clarification
- Genre indication and tone suggestion
- Length optimization and impact maximization
- Market appeal and executive attention capture

### Theme and Message
#### Thematic Foundation
- Central theme and philosophical core
- Moral question and ethical dilemma
- Social commentary and cultural reflection
- Personal journey and universal truth
- Contemporary issue and timeless value balance
- Character arc and theme reinforcement alignment

## 2. GENRE AND MARKET UNDERSTANDING

### Genre Analysis
#### Genre Conventions
- Classical genre element and essential component
- Audience expectation and satisfaction requirement

- Fresh approach and innovative interpretation
- Subgenre and hybrid genre exploration
- Market trend and commercial consideration

### Market Positioning
#### Commercial Viability
- Genre popularity and trend analysis
- Audience demographic and preference
- Platform and distribution channel
- Budget and production level
- International and global market potential
- Streaming and digital platform considerations

## 3. CHARACTER DEVELOPMENT PRINCIPLES

### Protagonist Design
#### Character Foundation
- Fundamental personality and character trait
- Background and formative experience
- Value system and moral framework
- Fear and insecurity exploration
- Desire and goal driving motivation
- Strength and capability demonstration
- Weakness and vulnerability portrayal

#### Character Arc
- Starting point and initial character state
- Catalyst and inciting incident impact
- Challenge and obstacle progression
- Learning and growth opportunity
- Breakthrough and revelation moment
- Final transformation and character completion

### Supporting Characters
#### Character Function
- Plot advancement and story progression
- Protagonist support and challenge provision
- Information and exposition delivery
- Relationship and connection definition
- Unique personality and distinctive trait
- Dialogue and voice distinctiveness

### Three-Act Structure
#### Act Structure Basics
- Act One setup and establishment
- Act Two development and complication
- Act Three climax and resolution
- Plot point timing and placement
- Midpoint and story direction shift
- Pacing and rhythm optimization

### Scene Organization
#### Scene Function
- Plot and story advancement
- Character and relationship development
- Information and exposition delivery
- Conflict and tension creation
- Theme and message reinforcement

### Voice Development

- Vocabulary and word choice selection
- Sentence structure and speaking pattern
- Regional dialect and accent consideration
- Education and background influence
- Personality and temperament reflection
- Individual quirk and speech characteristic

### Dialogue Craft
#### Writing Technique
- Subtext and underlying meaning
- Conflict and tension creation
- Information and exposition delivery
- Emotion and relationship development
- Authenticity and believability

- Character and emotion visual demonstration
- Information and exposition visual delivery
