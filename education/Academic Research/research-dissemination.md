# Research Dissemination and Publication Planning

## Purpose
Develop comprehensive dissemination strategies to effectively communicate research findings through academic publications, conference presentations, stakeholder engagement, and knowledge translation to maximize research impact and facilitate evidence-based practice and policy.

## 2. ACADEMIC PUBLICATION PLANNING

### Journal Selection
#### Target Journal Identification
- Discipline-specific journals
- Interdisciplinary journals
- Methodological journals
- Open-access journals
- High-impact vs. specialized journals
- Regional or international scope

#### Journal Evaluation Criteria
- Scope and fit with research
- Impact factor and citation metrics
- Acceptance rate
- Time to publication
- Open access options and fees
- Author rights and copyright
- Peer review process
- Readership and circulation
- Indexing (PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus)
- Reputation and credibility

### Manuscript Development
#### IMRAD Structure
**Introduction**
- Background and context
- Problem statement
- Literature review and gap
- Theoretical framework
- Research questions or hypotheses
- Study objectives and significance

**Methods**
- Research design and approach
- Participants and sampling
- Data collection procedures
- Instruments and measures
- Data analysis methods
- Ethical considerations

**Results**
- Sample characteristics
- Descriptive statistics
- Primary findings
- Secondary findings
- Tables and figures
- Objective reporting (no interpretation)

**Discussion**
- Summary of key findings
- Interpretation and meaning
- Comparison with existing literature
- Theoretical implications
- Practical implications
- Strengths and limitations
- Future research directions
- Conclusions

#### Abstract Development
- Structured vs. unstructured format
- Word limit adherence (typically 150-300 words)
- Key elements:
  * Background/Objective
  * Methods (brief)
  * Results (specific findings)
  * Conclusions/Implications
- Keywords selection (5-7 typically)
- Compelling and clear writing

#### Title Development
- Concise and descriptive (12-15 words ideal)
- Includes key variables or concepts
- Indicates study design (if appropriate)
- Avoids abbreviations
- Engaging but professional
- Searchable and SEO-friendly

### Writing Quality and Style
#### Clarity and Precision
- Active voice when appropriate
- Clear, concise sentences
- Logical organization and flow
- Appropriate technical language
- Define specialized terms
- Smooth transitions
- Consistent terminology

#### APA or Discipline-Specific Style
- Citation format compliance
- Reference list formatting
- Headings and subheadings
- Tables and figures
- Numbers and statistics reporting
- Abbreviations and symbols
- Bias-free language

#### Tables and Figures
- Clear titles and captions
- Self-explanatory
- High-quality resolution
- Appropriate type for data
- Consistent formatting
- Numbered sequentially
- Referenced in text
- Follow journal guidelines

### Submission Process
#### Pre-Submission Checklist
- Journal guidelines review
- Formatting compliance
- Complete manuscript components
- Author information and affiliations
- Conflicts of interest disclosure
- Funding acknowledgment
- IRB approval statement
- Data availability statement
- Author contributions (CRediT)
- Cover letter preparation

#### Peer Review Process
- Initial editorial screening
- Reviewer assignment (typically 2-3)
- Review period (2-8 weeks typically)
- Decision types:
  * Accept (rare at first submission)
  * Minor revisions
  * Major revisions
  * Reject and resubmit
  * Reject

#### Responding to Reviews
- Respectful and professional tone
- Point-by-point response
- Detailed explanation of changes
- Justification for non-changes
- Track changes in manuscript
- Response letter and revised manuscript
- Resubmission within deadline

### Open Access and Preprints
#### Open Access Options
- Gold OA: Journal is OA, often with APC (Article Processing Charge)
- Green OA: Self-archiving in repository (institutional or disciplinary)
- Hybrid OA: Pay for OA in subscription journal
- Bronze OA: Free to read but not openly licensed

#### Preprint Servers
- Benefits: Rapid dissemination, establish priority, feedback
- Servers: arXiv, bioRxiv, medRxiv, PsyArXiv, SocArXiv, SSRN
- Version control (update with revisions)
- Citation of preprint
- Journal policies on preprints

## 3. CONFERENCE PRESENTATIONS

### Conference Selection
#### Conference Types
- International, national, regional, local
- Disciplinary or interdisciplinary
- Specialized topic conferences
- Methodology-focused conferences
- Student or early-career conferences
- Invited vs. submitted presentations

#### Selection Criteria
- Audience fit and relevance
- Prestige and reputation
- Networking opportunities
- Location and travel costs
- Timing relative to study completion
- Abstract acceptance rate
- Presentation formats available
- Career advancement value

### Abstract Submission
#### Abstract Structure
- Title (attention-grabbing)
- Background/Introduction (brief context)
- Methods (design, sample, measures)
- Results (key findings with data)
- Conclusions/Implications
- Word limit compliance (typically 250-500)
- Keywords if required

#### Submission Process
- Meet deadline (typically 6-12 months before conference)
- Select submission category (oral, poster, symposium)
- Format according to guidelines
- Co-author approval
- Presentation preference indication
- Payment of submission fee (if applicable)

### Oral Presentation Development
#### Presentation Structure (10-20 minutes typical)
- Title slide (1 min)
- Introduction and background (2-3 min)
- Methods overview (2-3 min)
- Results (4-6 min)
- Discussion and implications (3-4 min)
- Conclusions and future directions (1-2 min)
- Acknowledgments and questions (1 min)

#### Slide Design Principles
- Minimal text (6 words per line, 6 lines per slide rule)
- High-quality visuals
- Consistent template and fonts
- Adequate font size (24-32 pt minimum)
- High contrast for readability
- Visual hierarchy
- One main idea per slide
- Data visualization (graphs, charts)
- Avoid clutter

#### Presentation Delivery
- Practice extensively (timing, flow)
- Engaging delivery style
- Eye contact with audience
- Clear, paced speaking
- Pointer or animation use (minimal)
- Backup plan (technology failure)
- Q&A preparation
- Handouts or QR code for details

### Poster Presentation Development
#### Poster Design Elements
- Standard size (typically 48" × 36" or 42" × 42")
- Logical layout (left to right, top to bottom)
- Large, readable fonts:
  * Title: 72-96 pt
  * Headings: 36-48 pt
  * Body text: 24-32 pt
- Visual appeal and whitespace
- Institutional branding
- Contact information
- QR code for more information

#### Poster Content
- Concise title
- Authors and affiliations
- Introduction (problem, gap, objectives)
- Methods (brief, visual)
- Results (graphs, tables, key findings)
- Conclusions and implications
- Future directions
- References (select few)
- Acknowledgments

#### Poster Session Strategy
- Prepare 1-2 minute "elevator pitch"
- Bring handouts or business cards
- Engage attendees actively
- Dress professionally
- Stand by poster during session
- Answer questions and discuss
- Collect contact information
- Network with interested attendees

## 4. STAKEHOLDER AND PUBLIC DISSEMINATION

### Policy Brief Development
#### Structure (2-4 pages)
- Executive summary (1 paragraph)
- Problem definition
- Policy context
- Research evidence summary
- Policy options or recommendations
- Implementation considerations
- Cost and feasibility
- References (select)
- Contact information

#### Writing Style
- Clear, jargon-free language
- Action-oriented recommendations
- Visual elements (boxes, bullets)
- Engaging and persuasive
- Evidence-based but accessible
- Concise and focused
- Timely and relevant

### Media Engagement
#### Press Releases
- Newsworthy angle
- Compelling headline
- Lead paragraph (who, what, when, where, why)
- Supporting details and quotes
- Institutional information
- Contact for media inquiries
- Release timing coordination

#### Media Interviews
- Key messages preparation (3-5 points)
- Bridging to main points
- Sound bites and quotable statements
- Plain language (no jargon)
- Examples and stories
- Stay on message
- Honesty about limitations
- Media training if possible

### Community Dissemination
#### Community Presentations
- Accessible language and content
- Interactive and engaging format
- Visual aids and demonstrations
- Cultural appropriateness
- Practical implications emphasis
- Q&A and discussion
- Feedback solicitation
- Action steps or resources

#### Community Reports
- Plain language summary
- Participatory design (if CBPR)
- Visual elements
- Actionable recommendations
- Community voice inclusion
- Cultural adaptation
- Multiple formats (print, digital, video)
- Community event for release

### Social Media and Digital Channels
#### Platform Selection
- Twitter/X: Brief updates, links, discussions
- LinkedIn: Professional networking, longer posts
- Facebook: Community engagement, events
- Instagram: Visual storytelling
- YouTube: Video presentations, tutorials
- ResearchGate/Academia.edu: Academic networking
- Blogs: Detailed explanations, commentary

#### Content Strategy
- Infographics and visual abstracts
- Short video summaries
- Plain language summaries
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Impact stories
- Engagement with followers
- Hashtags for visibility
- Regular posting schedule

## Usage Examples

### Example 1: Educational Research Dissemination
"Develop a dissemination plan for a mixed-methods study on peer mentoring in STEM education, targeting academic journals (Journal of STEM Education), regional and national education conferences, a policy brief for state education departments, and practitioner-focused webinars for university student affairs professionals."

### Example 2: Healthcare Research with Public Impact
"Create a comprehensive dissemination strategy for a clinical trial of a new diabetes management intervention, including submission to high-impact medical journals, presentations at diabetes conferences, development of clinical practice guidelines, patient education materials, media engagement for public awareness, and partnership with diabetes advocacy organizations."

### Example 3: Community-Based Research Translation
"Design a knowledge translation plan for participatory research on housing security in low-income communities, including community report-back events, policy briefs for city officials, presentations at community development conferences, practitioner toolkits, and ongoing partnership with community organizations for implementation."

## Best Practices

1. **Start planning early** - Dissemination strategy should be developed at study design phase
2. **Know your audiences** - Tailor messages and channels to specific groups
3. **Use multiple channels** - Diversify dissemination for broader reach
4. **Prioritize quality** - Well-crafted messages have greater impact
5. **Engage stakeholders** - Involve knowledge users in dissemination planning
6. **Be strategic** - Focus on high-impact opportunities
7. **Track and evaluate** - Monitor reach, engagement, and impact
8. **Build relationships** - Long-term partnerships enhance dissemination
9. **Invest in communication** - Allocate adequate time and resources
10. **Adapt and iterate** - Learn from experience and refine strategies

## Customization Options

1. **By Research Type**: Adapt for basic research, applied research, program evaluation, or policy analysis

2. **By Audience Priority**: Emphasize academic, practice, policy, or public dissemination

3. **By Career Stage**: Scale for student vs. faculty vs. established researcher dissemination

4. **By Resources**: Adjust for limited-budget vs. well-funded dissemination activities

5. **By Impact Goals**: Customize for knowledge advancement, practice change, policy influence, or public awareness objectives
