# M&A Strategy Framework

## Purpose
Develop comprehensive mergers and acquisitions strategy from strategic rationale through post-merger integration, ensuring acquisitions create value, achieve strategic objectives, and avoid common pitfalls that cause 70% of M&A deals to fail.

## Quick Start

**Want to evaluate an acquisition opportunity quickly?** Here's the minimal approach:

### When to Use This
- Evaluating whether to pursue acquisition-led growth
- Assessing specific acquisition targets
- Planning merger integration to capture synergies
- Considering divestiture of non-core assets
- Building inorganic growth strategy

### Minimal Example
```
Company: Mid-size SaaS company ($50M ARR)
Strategic Goal: Enter European market, acquire technology capability

Target: European CRM company ($10M ARR, strong EU customer base)

Quick Assessment:
Strategic Fit: HIGH (geographic expansion + tech capability)
Financial Fit: Purchase price $30M (3x revenue, reasonable)
Cultural Fit: MEDIUM (startup culture vs our corporate culture)
Integration Complexity: MEDIUM (different tech stack but similar business model)
Synergy Potential: $5M cost savings + $10M revenue synergies

Decision Factors:
✅ Strategic: Enters target market with established customer base
✅ Financial: Accretive in Year 2, IRR 25%+
⚠️ Risk: Integration execution, customer retention
⚠️ Culture: Will require active culture integration

Decision: PROCEED with detailed due diligence, prepare integration plan
```

### Basic 5-Step Workflow
1. **Define M&A Strategy** - Why acquire, what capabilities to build/buy
2. **Screen & Identify Targets** - Find companies that fit criteria
3. **Evaluate & Value** - Deep due diligence and valuation
4. **Structure & Negotiate** - Deal terms and price
5. **Integrate & Realize Value** - Execute integration plan

**Time to complete**: 2-4 weeks for strategy, 3-6 months for deal execution

**Pro tip**: Integration planning should start during due diligence, not after close

## PHASE 5: INTEGRATION PLANNING & EXECUTION

### 5.1 Integration Strategy

**Integration Approach:**

**Preservation** (Hands-off)
- When: Acquired for growth, fragile culture, different business model
- Approach: Operate independently, minimal integration
- Example: Facebook/Instagram (initially)

**Absorption** (Full integration)
- When: Cost synergies primary driver, similar operations
- Approach: Fold into existing structure, one operating model
- Example: Most horizontal acquisitions

**Symbiosis** (Best of both)
- When: Mutual benefits, complementary strengths
- Approach: Selective integration, share capabilities
- Example: Disney/Pixar

**Holding** (Portfolio)
- When: Financial investment, unrelated business
- Approach: Financial oversight only
- Example: Berkshire Hathaway model

## Best Practices

### Do's ✅
- **Start with Strategy**: Know why you're acquiring before what to acquire
- **Invest in Due Diligence**: Most failures trace to inadequate diligence
- **Plan Integration Early**: Start Day 1 planning during diligence
- **Communicate Constantly**: Over-communicate during integration
- **Retain Key Talent**: People make deals work or fail
- **Track Synergies**: Measure what you promised
- **Learn and Improve**: Capture lessons for next deal

### Don'ts ❌
- **Don't Overpay**: Deal fever causes winner's curse
- **Don't Neglect Culture**: Culture clash kills deals
- **Don't Assume Integration is Easy**: It's always harder than expected
- **Don't Ignore Customer Risk**: Customers leave during disruption
- **Don't Rush Integration**: Balance speed with thoughtfulness
- **Don't Forget to Communicate**: Silence creates fear

---

## Common M&A Pitfalls

1. **Strategic Drift**: Acquisition doesn't fit strategy
2. **Winner's Curse**: Overpayment in competitive auction
3. **Integration Failure**: Poor execution of integration
4. **Culture Clash**: Incompatible cultures not addressed
5. **Key Talent Loss**: Critical people leave
6. **Customer Defection**: Customers lost in transition
7. **Synergy Mirage**: Overestimated synergies
8. **Hidden Liabilities**: Due diligence missed issues
