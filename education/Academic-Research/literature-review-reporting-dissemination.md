# Literature Review Reporting & Dissemination

## Purpose
Prepare comprehensive literature review reports following international standards, develop targeted dissemination strategies for diverse audiences, create accessible communication products, and plan for knowledge translation and real-world implementation to maximize research impact.

## Quick Start

### For Authors & Knowledge Mobilizers

**Step 1: Prepare Manuscript (Variable)**
- Select target journal and review submission guidelines
- Follow PRISMA or relevant reporting checklist
- Write structured abstract and main manuscript
- Prepare tables, figures, and supplementary materials

**Step 2: Plan Dissemination Strategy**
- Identify target audiences and stakeholder groups
- Select appropriate dissemination channels
- Develop audience-specific messaging
- Create communication timeline and milestones

**Step 3: Create Communication Products (Variable)**
- Develop plain language summary for public
- Create policy brief for decision-makers
- Prepare conference presentations and posters
- Design infographics and visual summaries

**Step 4: Implement and Evaluate (Ongoing)**
- Execute dissemination plan across channels
- Engage stakeholders through multiple touchpoints
- Monitor reach and impact metrics
- Adapt strategy based on feedback and uptake

**Common Use Cases:**
- Journal manuscript submission
- Conference presentation preparation
- Policy brief development
- Media engagement and press releases
- Stakeholder webinars and workshops

## Best Practices

1. **Follow reporting guidelines rigorously** - Complete PRISMA or relevant checklist before submission
2. **Tailor messaging to audiences** - Different stakeholders need different information
3. **Use multiple dissemination channels** - Maximize reach through diverse platforms
4. **Create visual summaries** - Infographics and figures enhance understanding
5. **Engage stakeholders early** - Co-design communication products for relevance
6. **Plan for accessibility** - Ensure materials work for people with disabilities
7. **Provide plain language versions** - Academic jargon limits broader impact
8. **Build partnerships for amplification** - Leverage networks for wider reach
9. **Monitor and evaluate impact** - Track metrics to assess effectiveness
10. **Plan for sustainability** - Think beyond initial publication to long-term impact

## Tips for Success

- Create dissemination plan alongside the review protocol
- Budget time and resources for knowledge translation activities
- Identify "champions" who can amplify your message
- Use storytelling and narratives to make findings memorable
- Leverage existing networks and platforms rather than building from scratch
- Consider timing for maximum impact (policy windows, conference cycles)
- Create reusable templates and materials for efficiency
- Build evaluation into dissemination from the start
- Celebrate and showcase real-world applications
- Maintain relationships with stakeholders for future collaboration
