# Financial Analysis - Overview & Navigation

## Purpose
Navigate the comprehensive suite of financial analysis templates to select the right tool for your specific analysis needs.

## Quick Start

**Want to analyze a company's financials quickly?** Here's how to choose the right template:

### When to Use This Overview
- Evaluating investment opportunities or acquisition targets
- Conducting due diligence on companies
- Assessing financial health for lending or credit decisions
- Preparing board reports or investor presentations
- Need to understand which financial analysis to perform

### Quick Template Selection
```
Your Analysis Goal → Recommended Template:

1. Understand overall financial performance and trends
   → financial-statement-analysis.md (Income statement, balance sheet, cash flow, 30-45 min)

2. Calculate key metrics and benchmark against competitors
   → financial-ratio-analysis.md (Liquidity, profitability, leverage ratios, 20-30 min)

3. Project future financial performance
   → financial-forecasting.md (Revenue, expense, cash flow projections, 1-2 hours)

4. Assess financial risks and sustainability
   → financial-risk-assessment.md (Credit risk, liquidity risk, operational risk, 45-60 min)

5. Determine company value for M&A or investment
   → financial-valuation-recommendations.md (DCF, comps, multiples valuation, 2-3 hours)
```

### Basic 3-Step Workflow
1. **Start with statement analysis** - Use financial-statement-analysis.md to understand historical performance
2. **Calculate key ratios** - Use financial-ratio-analysis.md to benchmark and identify strengths/weaknesses
3. **Add depth as needed** - Use forecasting for future projections, risk assessment for red flags, or valuation for M&A

**Time to complete**: 1-2 hours for basic analysis, 1 day for comprehensive financial evaluation

**Pro tip**: For initial screening, use financial-statement-analysis.md + financial-ratio-analysis.md. This covers 80% of what you need to know before deeper analysis.

## Template Suite Overview

The financial analysis suite consists of 5 specialized, focused templates, each designed for a specific analysis purpose:

### 1. Financial Statement Analysis
**File:** `financial-statement-analysis.md`

**Use this when you need to:**
- Analyze income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements
- Evaluate historical financial performance
- Assess financial health and sustainability
- Understand revenue, profitability, and cash generation trends
- Identify working capital efficiency

**Key outputs:**
- Income statement analysis (revenue, margins, profitability)
- Balance sheet analysis (assets, liabilities, equity)
- Cash flow analysis (operating, investing, financing)
- Financial health assessment
- Trend identification

**Typical use cases:**
- Quarterly/annual performance reviews
- Credit analysis and lending decisions
- Initial due diligence
- Financial health checkups
- Management reporting

---

### 2. Financial Ratio Analysis
**File:** `financial-ratio-analysis.md`

- Peer comparison analysis
- Performance benchmarking
- Credit rating assessments
- Operational efficiency evaluation

## Selection Guide

### Quick Decision Tree

**What is your primary objective?**

1. **Understand current/historical performance?**
   → Use **Financial Statement Analysis**

2. **Benchmark against peers or industry?**
   → Use **Financial Ratio Analysis**

3. **Project future performance?**
   → Use **Financial Forecasting**

4. **Assess risks and downside scenarios?**
   → Use **Financial Risk Assessment**

5. **Determine company value or make investment decision?**
   → Use **Financial Valuation & Recommendations**

### Common Workflows

**Complete Company Analysis:**
Use templates in sequence:
1. Financial Statement Analysis (understand historical performance)
2. Financial Ratio Analysis (benchmark and compare)
3. Financial Forecasting (project future performance)
4. Financial Risk Assessment (identify risks)
5. Financial Valuation & Recommendations (determine value and recommend)

**Investment Decision:**
1. Financial Statement Analysis
2. Financial Ratio Analysis
3. Financial Valuation & Recommendations
4. Financial Risk Assessment

**Credit Assessment:**
1. Financial Statement Analysis
2. Financial Ratio Analysis
3. Financial Risk Assessment
4. Financial Forecasting (to assess debt service capability)

**Strategic Planning:**
1. Financial Statement Analysis
2. Financial Forecasting
3. Financial Risk Assessment

**M&A Due Diligence:**
1. Financial Statement Analysis
2. Financial Ratio Analysis
3. Financial Risk Assessment

## Best Practices

### General Guidelines
1. **Start with historical analysis** before projecting forward
2. **Use multiple templates together** for comprehensive analysis
3. **Document all assumptions** clearly
4. **Update regularly** as new information becomes available
5. **Compare to benchmarks** (industry, peers, historical)
6. **Consider context** (economic conditions, industry dynamics)
7. **Focus on trends**, not just point-in-time metrics
8. **Validate outputs** for reasonableness

### Data Requirements
All templates require:
- Financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow)
- Industry and peer data for benchmarking
- Clear understanding of business model and operations
- Market and economic context

Additional requirements vary by template:
- **Forecasting:** Historical trends, strategic plans, market data
- **Risk Assessment:** Risk factors, stress scenarios
- **Valuation:** Peer trading data, transaction comps, discount rates

### Quality Checks
Before finalizing any analysis:
- ✓ All calculations verified
- ✓ Assumptions documented and reasonable
- ✓ Benchmarks and comparisons included
- ✓ Trends and drivers identified
- ✓ Risks and uncertainties noted
- ✓ Conclusions supported by data
- ✓ Recommendations are actionable
