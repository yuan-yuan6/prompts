# Workout Programs Template

## Overview
This comprehensive template assists fitness professionals, healthcare providers, and individuals in designing evidence-based, progressive workout programs that optimize physical performance, health outcomes, and injury prevention while accommodating individual capabilities and goals.

## Usage Examples

### Example 1: Beginner Strength Training Program
```
As a Certified Personal Trainer with ACSM CPT working in Commercial Gym with 6 years of experience, I am designing a workout program for 35-year-old Female Caucasian with General fitness and strength building.

Physical Assessment Data: Height 5'5", Weight 140 lbs, BMI 23.3, Body fat percentage 28%, Muscle mass 38%, No previous injuries.

Program Overview:
Program name: Foundation Strength Builder
Duration: 12 weeks
Phases: Adaptation (4 weeks), Strength Building (6 weeks), Consolidation (2 weeks)
Training frequency: 3 sessions per week
Session duration: 45 minutes

Training Split: Full Body
Monday: Full Body Strength
- Primary muscle groups: All major muscle groups
- Session type: Compound movement focus
- Duration: 45 minutes
- Intensity level: Moderate (65-75% effort)

Exercise 1: Goblet Squat
- Sets: 3 x Reps: 10-12
- Load: 15-25 lbs dumbbell
- Rest: 60-90 seconds
- Tempo: 3-1-2-1 (controlled descent, pause, explosive up, pause)
- Progression: Add 2.5-5 lbs every 2 weeks
```

### Example 2: Athletic Performance Program for Basketball Player

```
As a Strength and Conditioning Specialist with CSCS working in Athletic Performance Center with 10 years of experience, I am designing a workout program for 22-year-old Male African-American with Basketball performance enhancement and injury prevention.

Physical Assessment Data: Height 6'3", Weight 195 lbs, BMI 24.4, Body fat percentage 8%, VO2 max 58 ml/kg/min, Vertical jump 32 inches.

Program Overview:
Program name: Basketball Performance Optimization

Training frequency: 5 sessions per week
Session duration: 75-90 minutes

Periodization Model: Block Periodization
- Anatomical Adaptation Block (4 weeks)
- Maximum Strength Block (4 weeks)  
- Power Development Block (4 weeks)
- Competition Preparation Block (4 weeks)

Primary Compound Movements:
Exercise 1: Back Squat
- Sets: 4 x Reps: 3-5

- Rest: 3-4 minutes
- Tempo: 3-1-X-1 (controlled descent, pause, explosive up, pause)
- Progression: Linear load progression
```

## Customization Options

### 1. Goal-Specific Programs
- **Weight Loss**: High-volume training, metabolic circuits, and cardiovascular emphasis
- **Muscle Building**: Progressive overload focus, hypertrophy rep ranges, and adequate recovery
- **Strength Development**: Low-rep heavy lifting, compound movement focus, and neurological adaptation
- **Athletic Performance**: Sport-specific movements, power development, and periodized training
- **Functional Fitness**: Movement pattern emphasis, real-world applications, and daily activity integration

### 2. Experience Level Adaptations
- **Beginner Programs**: Basic movement patterns, lower intensity, and education focus
- **Intermediate Programs**: Increased complexity, moderate to high intensity, and skill development
- **Advanced Programs**: High-intensity techniques, specialized methods, and performance optimization
- **Expert/Athlete Programs**: Sport-specific periodization, advanced techniques, and competition preparation
- **Return to Exercise**: Gradual progression, medical considerations, and conservative approaches

### 3. Equipment and Setting Variations
- **Home Workouts**: Minimal equipment, bodyweight focus, and space-efficient exercises
- **Commercial Gym**: Full equipment access, machine and free weight integration, and group fitness options
- **Outdoor Training**: Natural movement patterns, weather considerations, and minimal equipment needs
- **Corporate Fitness**: Time constraints, professional environment, and stress management focus
- **Specialized Facilities**: Sport-specific equipment, advanced monitoring, and expert supervision

### 4. Health Condition Modifications
- **Cardiovascular Conditions**: Heart rate monitoring, medical supervision, and gradual progression
- **Musculoskeletal Injuries**: Rehabilitation focus, movement modification, and pain management
- **Metabolic Disorders**: Exercise timing considerations, intensity modifications, and medical coordination
- **Mental Health Support**: Mood enhancement focus, stress reduction, and social interaction benefits
