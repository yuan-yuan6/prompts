## EPISODE SCRIPT TEMPLATE

### Opening (2-3 minutes)
```
[THEME MUSIC: 10 seconds]

HOST:
[Energetic greeting and introduction]
"Welcome to [Podcast Name], the show where [value proposition].
I'm your host [Name], and today we're diving into [topic]."

[Quick hook - why listener should care]
"If you've ever wondered [question], or struggled with [problem],
this episode is for you."

[Guest introduction if applicable]
"I'm joined by [Guest Name], who [credentials and relevance]."

[Episode preview]
"In the next [duration], we'll cover [key points]."

[Sponsor mention if applicable]
"But first, a quick word from our sponsor..."

[TRANSITION MUSIC: 5 seconds]
```

### Main Content (70-80% of episode)
```
SEGMENT 1: Topic Introduction (5-7 minutes)
HOST:
[Set context and background]
[Explain why topic matters]
[Share personal connection or story]

GUEST (if interview):
[Initial thoughts and perspective]
[Key insight or story]

SEGMENT 2: Deep Dive (10-15 minutes)
[Explore main concepts in detail]
[Use examples and case studies]
[Address common questions or misconceptions]

SEGMENT 3: Practical Application (8-12 minutes)
[Actionable tips and strategies]
[Step-by-step guidance]
[Resources and tools]

[MID-ROLL AD: 60 seconds if applicable]

SEGMENT 4: Additional Insights (5-10 minutes)
[Advanced topics or alternatives]
[Common challenges and solutions]
[Future trends and predictions]
```

### Closing (2-3 minutes)
```
HOST:
[Recap key takeaways]
"So to recap, we covered [main points]..."

[Thank guest if applicable]
"[Guest Name], thank you so much for sharing [specific insight]."

GUEST:
"Thanks for having me, it was great to discuss [topic]."

HOST:
[Call-to-action]
"If you found this valuable, please [subscribe/rate/review/share]."

[Social media and website]
"Connect with us at [platforms] and visit [website] for show notes."

[Next episode tease]
"Next week, we're talking about [topic] with [guest]."

[Final branding]
"Until then, [catchphrase or sign-off]."

[OUTRO MUSIC: 15 seconds]
```

## INTERVIEW QUESTION FRAMEWORK

### Opening Questions (Build Rapport)
- "Tell us a bit about your background and how you got into [field]"
- "What's been keeping you busy lately?"
- "What inspired you to [achievement/project]?"

### Core Content Questions (Extract Value)
- "Can you walk us through [process/experience]?"
- "What's the biggest misconception about [topic]?"
- "If someone is struggling with [problem], what would you tell them?"
- "What surprised you most when [experience]?"

### Deep Dive Questions (Go Deeper)
- "Can you give us a specific example of [concept]?"
- "How do you handle [challenge] in your work?"
- "What's a contrarian view you hold about [topic]?"
- "What don't people realize about [subject]?"

### Forward-Looking Questions (Future Focus)
- "Where do you see [industry/field] heading?"
- "What advice would you give to someone starting out?"
- "What's next for you and your work?"
- "What resources would you recommend?"

### Closing Questions (Memorable Ending)
- "What's the one thing you want listeners to remember?"
- "Where can people find your work and connect with you?"
- "Is there anything we didn't cover that you'd like to mention?"

## SCRIPTING APPROACHES

### Fully Scripted (Word-for-Word)
**Best for**:
- Solo narrative podcasts
- Polished, professional delivery
- Time-sensitive content
- Sponsors and ads

**Pros**: Precise, efficient, error-free
**Cons**: Can sound stiff, less spontaneous

### Bullet Point Outline
**Best for**:
- Interviews and conversations
- Co-hosted shows
- Flexible discussions
- Natural delivery

**Pros**: Natural, authentic, adaptable
**Cons**: May ramble, time management harder

### Hybrid Approach
**Best for**:
- Most podcast formats
- Balanced professionalism and authenticity

**Structure**:
- Scripted: Intro, outro, transitions, sponsor reads
- Outlined: Main content, interview questions
- Improvised: Reactions, follow-ups, banter

## Best Practices

1. **Write how you speak** - Scripts should sound natural when read aloud
2. **Front-load value** - Hook listeners in first 30 seconds
3. **Use short sentences** - Easier to deliver and understand
4. **Build in pauses** - Allow ideas to land with audience
5. **Prepare more than needed** - Have backup questions and content
6. **Read aloud before recording** - Test flow and timing
7. **Time your segments** - Ensure episode fits target length
8. **Mark energy levels** - Note where to increase or decrease intensity
9. **Include production notes** - Guide for editing and sound design
10. **Stay flexible** - Be ready to adapt during recording

## Tips for Success

- Practice reading your script multiple times before recording
- Have water nearby and take hydration breaks
- Mark difficult words or phrases phonetically
- Include emotional direction in parentheticals
- Build in spontaneity moments - don't over-script
- Keep guest comfortable with pre-interview chat
- Record a few different versions of key sections
- Save ad-libs and spontaneous moments - often the best content
- Review and refine script based on past episode performance
- Create a script template to streamline future episodes
