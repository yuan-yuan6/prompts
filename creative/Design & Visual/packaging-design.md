## Best Practices

1. **Design with structure in mind** - Work with dieline from the start
2. **Test physical mockups** - Create white dummy before printing
3. **Consider manufacturing constraints** - Verify die-cutting and assembly feasibility
4. **Optimize for production method** - Design appropriate for printing technique
5. **Plan for variants** - Create flexible system for multiple SKUs
6. **Test at shelf** - Mock up in retail environment for visibility
7. **Verify all regulations** - Double-check compliance requirements
8. **Prototype unboxing** - Test consumer experience before production
9. **Calculate cost early** - Factor in materials, printing, and finishing
10. **Specify sustainability** - Document environmental commitments

## Tips for Success

- Visit retail stores to study category and competition
- Create physical mockups to test structure and proportions
- Design artwork with bleed and safety margins
- Specify Pantone colors for accurate matching
- Test barcode scanning from printed sample
- Consider global markets and multi-language needs
- Plan for future line extensions and brand growth
- Work with packaging engineer for complex structures
- Request material samples before finalizing
- Build in timeline for regulatory review and approval
