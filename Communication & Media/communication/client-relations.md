# Client Relations & Account Management Readiness Assessment

## Purpose
Assess readiness to build **lasting, value-driven client relationships** that maximize retention, expansion, and advocacy. Use this to diagnose gaps in account strategy, engagement, issue resolution, and growth execution.

## Template

Conduct a client relations & account management readiness assessment for {CLIENT_CONTEXT}.

Score each dimension **1â€“5** (1 = ad hoc, 5 = optimized). Ground findings in observable signals (health scores, engagement patterns, retention data, expansion pipeline).

**1) ACCOUNT STRATEGY & SEGMENTATION**
- Clients are segmented by value, complexity, and strategic importance
- Account plans exist with goals, stakeholders, risks, and growth opportunities
- Success criteria and value metrics are co-defined with the client
- Resources (AMs, SEs, execs) are aligned to account tier and need

**2) RELATIONSHIP MAPPING & STAKEHOLDER ENGAGEMENT**
- Key stakeholders (economic buyer, champion, influencers) are identified
- Engagement cadence matches client preference and relationship maturity
- Executive sponsors are active in strategic accounts
- Relationship depth extends beyond single point of contact

**3) ONBOARDING & VALUE REALIZATION**
- Onboarding plans set clear milestones, timelines, and success measures
- Time-to-value is tracked and optimized
- Early wins and adoption checkpoints build momentum
- Handoff from sales to account management is seamless

**4) PROACTIVE ENGAGEMENT & BUSINESS REVIEWS**
- Quarterly or bi-annual business reviews (QBRs/EBRs) are planned and executed
- Reviews demonstrate ROI, highlight usage trends, and identify opportunities
- Communication is proactive (not just reactive to issues)
- Account health is monitored via usage, satisfaction, and engagement signals

**5) ISSUE RESOLUTION & ESCALATION**
- Issues are triaged by severity and escalated with clear ownership
- Response SLAs exist and are met consistently
- Root cause analysis prevents repeat issues
- Client trust is maintained through transparency and follow-through

**6) RETENTION & EXPANSION EXECUTION**
- Renewal pipeline is tracked with risk/health scoring
- Churn signals (usage decline, satisfaction drop, disengagement) trigger intervention
- Expansion plays (upsell, cross-sell, new use cases) are identified and pursued
- Client feedback drives product and service improvements

## Best Practices (Exactly 8)
1) Segment accounts by strategic value and tailor engagement intensity accordingly.
2) Multi-thread relationships early; never be single-threaded in strategic accounts.
3) Define success metrics collaboratively with the client at kickoff and revisit quarterly.
4) Track leading indicators of churn (usage trends, sentiment, engagement drop) not just lagging.
5) Conduct QBRs that demonstrate ROI and uncover expansion opportunities, not just status updates.
6) Respond to issues transparently with ownership, timelines, and follow-through.
7) Map renewals 90+ days in advance; treat every renewal as an expansion opportunity.
8) Build executive sponsor relationships for strategic accounts to enable long-term partnership.
