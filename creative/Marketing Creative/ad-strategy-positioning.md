# Ad Strategy & Positioning Template

## Purpose
Develop strategic foundation for advertising campaigns including audience analysis, competitive positioning, pain point identification, and messaging framework.

## Usage Examples

### Example 1: B2B Software Campaign
**Context**: Enterprise CRM software targeting sales managers
**Input**:
- PRIMARY_AUDIENCE: "Sales managers and operations directors at mid-sized companies"
- PAIN_POINTS: "Manual data entry, lost leads, poor sales visibility"
- UNIQUE_VALUE_PROPOSITION: "50% faster sales cycle with automated lead scoring"
- COMPETITIVE_ADVANTAGE: "AI-powered insights, seamless integrations, proven ROI"

### Example 2: Consumer Brand Repositioning
**Context**: Sustainable fashion brand targeting millennials
**Input**:
- PRIMARY_AUDIENCE: "Environmentally conscious women 25-40"
- EMOTIONAL_APPEAL: "Style with purpose, guilt-free fashion choices"
- BRAND_VALUES: "Sustainability, ethical manufacturing, accessible luxury"
- DIFFERENTIATION: "Transparent supply chain, carbon-neutral shipping"

## Best Practices

1. **Conduct deep audience research** before positioning decisions
2. **Validate assumptions** with data and user testing
3. **Differentiate authentically** based on real strengths
4. **Balance emotion and logic** appropriate to audience and category
5. **Test positioning** with target audience before full launch
6. **Document clearly** for consistent execution
7. **Monitor competitors** continuously for emerging threats
8. **Measure effectiveness** against defined success metrics
9. **Iterate based on performance** while maintaining strategic consistency
10. **Align stakeholders** early on positioning decisions

## Tips for Success

- Start with qualitative research to understand audience deeply
- Use customer language and insights in messaging development
- Create clear differentiation from competitors
- Ensure positioning is ownable and defensible
- Build flexibility for platform-specific adaptation
- Maintain consistency while allowing creative expression
- Test early and often with real audience members
- Document decisions and rationale for team alignment
