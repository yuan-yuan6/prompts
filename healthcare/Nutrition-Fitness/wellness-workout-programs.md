# Workout Programs - Comprehensive Prompt Template

## Introduction
This comprehensive prompt template enables fitness professionals, personal trainers, coaches, and individuals to design detailed, scientifically-based workout programs incorporating exercise science, progressive overload, periodization, and individualized programming across all fitness levels and goals.

## Quick Start

**Get started in 3 steps:**

1. **Assess Fitness Baseline** - Evaluate current fitness level ([CURRENT_FITNESS_LEVEL]), movement quality ([MOVEMENT_SCREENING]), health history ([MEDICAL_CONDITIONS]), and training goals ([PRIMARY_GOALS]) to establish safe starting point.

2. **Design Program Structure** - Select training split ([TRAINING_SPLIT]), determine weekly frequency, set volume parameters ([TOTAL_SETS], [REPS_PER_SET]), and choose appropriate exercises for individual needs and goals.

3. **Implement Progressive Overload** - Establish clear progression strategy ([PROGRESSION_STRATEGIES]), track performance metrics ([TRAINING_LOG]), and adjust intensity/volume based on recovery and adaptation.

**Example:** "Create 12-week strength program for intermediate lifter: 4-day upper/lower split, compound movements, progressive overload targeting 10% strength gains, includes mobility work and recovery protocols."

### Example 1: Beginner Strength Training Program
**Context**: 30-year-old office worker starting resistance training

**Implementation**:
- Assessment: Sedentary lifestyle, poor posture, no training experience, BMI 28
- Program: 3x/week full body, focus on movement patterns, 8-week foundation phase
- Exercises: Goblet squats, push-ups, assisted pull-ups, planks, walking
- Progression: Master bodyweight before adding load, focus on form over weight
- Recovery: 48 hours between sessions, emphasis on sleep and walking on off days

### Example 2: Marathon Training Plan
**Context**: Experienced runner preparing for first marathon

**Implementation**:
- Current fitness: 10k PR 45 minutes, running 25 miles/week
- Program: 16-week build, 4 runs/week plus cross-training
- Periodization: Base building, strength phase, peak phase, taper
- Key workouts: Long runs to 20 miles, tempo runs, interval training, easy runs
- Support: Strength training 2x/week, yoga 1x/week, nutrition periodization

### Example 3: Athletic Performance Enhancement
**Context**: High school basketball player improving vertical jump

**Implementation**:
- Baseline: 24-inch vertical, needs explosive power and reactive strength
- Program: 12-week power development, 4x/week training
- Focus: Plyometrics, Olympic lift variations, strength base, jump mechanics
- Progression: Strength phase → Power phase → Reactive phase → Competition prep
- Testing: Vertical jump, broad jump, 10-yard sprint every 4 weeks

## Customization Options

### Option 1: Powerlifting Focus
Emphasize maximal strength in squat, bench, deadlift with competition peaking, technical mastery, and accessory work for weak points.

### Option 2: Bodybuilding Approach
Prioritize muscle hypertrophy, symmetry, proportion, and definition with high volume, varied exercises, and body part splits.

### Option 3: CrossFit Methodology
Integrate constantly varied functional movements at high intensity with Olympic lifting, gymnastics, and metabolic conditioning.

### Option 4: Endurance Specialization
Focus on aerobic development, lactate threshold, running economy, and race-specific preparation with structured periodization.

### Option 5: Functional Fitness
Emphasize real-world movement patterns, core stability, balance, and activities of daily living for general health and wellness.
