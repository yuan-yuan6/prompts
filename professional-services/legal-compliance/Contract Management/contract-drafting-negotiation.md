## Best Practices

1. **Prepare thoroughly** - Research counterparty, market conditions, and alternatives
2. **Set clear objectives** - Know your must-haves, nice-to-haves, and walk-away points
3. **Build relationships** - Focus on long-term partnership, not just transaction
4. **Listen actively** - Understand counterparty's interests and constraints
5. **Create value** - Look for win-win solutions and creative alternatives
6. **Document everything** - Keep detailed records of all negotiations and agreements
7. **Stay professional** - Maintain ethical standards and professional conduct
8. **Be flexible** - Adapt strategy based on negotiation dynamics and information

## Tips for Success

- Start with a strong but reasonable opening position
- Use silence strategically to gather information and create pressure
- Focus on interests rather than positions to find creative solutions
- Build in flexibility through contingent terms and phase-based approaches
- Maintain credibility through consistent messaging and follow-through
- Use objective criteria and market benchmarks to justify positions
- Know when to escalate and when to walk away
- Celebrate agreement and begin relationship building immediately
