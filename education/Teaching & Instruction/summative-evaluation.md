## SUMMATIVE ASSESSMENT TYPES

### Final Examinations
**Comprehensive Exam**:
- Covers all course material
- Multiple question formats
- Cognitive levels represented
- 2-3 hours typically
- Controlled environment
- High-stakes nature

**Cumulative Project**:
- Demonstrates integrated learning
- Applied knowledge and skills
- Individual or group format
- Extended timeline
- Real-world application
- Portfolio submission

### Performance Assessments
**Demonstrations**:
- Skill performance in authentic context
- Observed and evaluated
- Rubric-scored
- May include self-assessment
- Video recording option
- Multiple attempt possibility

**Presentations**:
- Oral communication assessment
- Visual aid integration
- Q&A component
- Peer and instructor evaluation
- Individual or group format
- Professional standards

### Portfolio Assessments
**Showcase Portfolio**:
- Best work selection
- Student choice emphasized
- Reflection required
- Growth demonstrated
- Professional presentation
- Future goals included

**Documentation Portfolio**:
- Comprehensive work collection
- Chronological or thematic organization
- Process and product included
- Regular entries required
- Progress tracking evident
- Learning journey visible

### Major Projects
**Research Project**:
- Original investigation
- Literature review
- Methodology and analysis
- Conclusions and recommendations
- Academic writing standards
- Citation and references

**Creative Project**:
- Original work creation
- Design and implementation
- Artistic or innovative expression
- Technical proficiency
- Conceptual depth
- Presentation and defense

**Capstone Project**:
- Integrative final experience
- Applies all course learning
- Professional quality expected
- Authentic audience/purpose
- Significant time investment
- Public presentation

## Best Practices

1. **Align with outcomes** - Assess what you taught
2. **Use multiple measures** - Don't rely on single assessment
3. **Provide clear criteria** - Students should know expectations
4. **Design for validity** - Measure what matters
5. **Ensure reliability** - Consistent scoring across students
6. **Offer appropriate challenge** - Not too easy or too hard
7. **Allow adequate time** - Don't rush students
8. **Consider accessibility** - Design for all learners
9. **Plan for feedback** - Even summative can inform future learning
10. **Review and improve** - Use data to refine assessments

## Tips for Success

- Create comprehensive assessment blueprints
- Develop high-quality rubrics in advance
- Pilot test assessments when possible
- Provide study guides and review sessions
- Offer sample questions or exemplars
- Build in multiple opportunities to demonstrate learning
- Use technology to streamline grading
- Analyze results to improve teaching and assessment
- Maintain item banks for test security
- Balance rigor with fairness
