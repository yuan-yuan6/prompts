## Usage Examples

### Example 1: E-commerce Campaign Optimization
**Context**: Improving ROAS for product ads
**Input**:
- CURRENT_METRICS: "ROAS 3.5x, CTR 2.1%, CVR 3.2%"
- OPTIMIZATION_OBJECTIVES: "Increase ROAS to 5x"
- TESTING_BUDGET: "$10,000 for testing over 6 weeks"

**Testing Plan**:
1. Week 1-2: Headline testing (5 variations)
2. Week 3-4: Image vs. video creative
3. Week 5-6: Landing page optimization
4. Continuous: Bid strategy and audience refinement

### Example 2: Lead Generation Optimization
**Context**: B2B software lead quality improvement
**Input**:
- CURRENT_METRICS: "CPA $85, Lead quality score 6.2/10"
- OPTIMIZATION_OBJECTIVES: "Reduce CPA to $65, increase quality to 8/10"

**Approach**:
- Test audience targeting refinement
- Optimize lead form length and fields
- Test value proposition messaging
- Implement lead scoring integration

## Best Practices

1. **Test one variable at a time** for clear attribution
2. **Reach statistical significance** before making decisions
3. **Document all tests** including failures for learning
4. **Set clear success criteria** before testing
5. **Account for external factors** in analysis
6. **Test continuously** rather than one-off experiments
7. **Validate results** across different segments
8. **Scale winners gradually** to confirm performance
9. **Share learnings** across teams and campaigns
10. **Balance short-term wins** with long-term strategy

## Tips for Success

- Run tests for full business cycles (weekly, monthly)
- Ensure sufficient sample size for reliable results
- Watch for novelty effects in winning variants
- Test big swings alongside incremental improvements
- Use holdout groups to measure incrementality
- Combine quantitative data with qualitative insights
- Build a testing culture, not just test execution
- Invest in proper tracking and analytics infrastructure
- Don't stop testing when performance is good
- Challenge assumptions regularly with new tests
