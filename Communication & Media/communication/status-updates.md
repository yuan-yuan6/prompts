# Status Updates & Progress Reporting Readiness Assessment

## Purpose
Assess readiness to deliver **clear, timely, and actionable status updates** that keep stakeholders informed, aligned, and able to make decisions. Use this to diagnose gaps in format, cadence, and issue escalation.

## Template

Conduct a status updates & progress reporting readiness assessment for {REPORTING_CONTEXT}.

Score each dimension **1â€“5** (1 = ad hoc, 5 = optimized). Ground findings in observable signals (stakeholder satisfaction, decision velocity, issue resolution speed, rework frequency).

**1) CLARITY & STRUCTURE**
- Overall status (Red/Yellow/Green) is stated upfront with one-sentence summary
- Format is consistent and scannable (3-3-3: accomplishments, in-progress, upcoming)
- Key information is highlighted (not buried in paragraphs)
- Jargon is minimized; audience-appropriate detail level is used

**2) TIMELINESS & CADENCE**
- Reports are delivered on schedule (not late)
- Cadence matches stakeholder needs and project phase (daily/weekly/milestone-based)
- Ad hoc updates happen when critical issues emerge
- Reporting rhythm is predictable and reliable

**3) ISSUE & RISK TRANSPARENCY**
- Blockers and risks are flagged explicitly (not hidden)
- Each issue includes: description, impact, owner, and resolution plan/date
- Escalation criteria are clear and followed
- Stakeholders trust that problems surface early

**4) METRICS & EVIDENCE**
- Progress is quantified (% complete, velocity, burn rate, milestones)
- Trends are shown (not just point-in-time snapshots)
- Actuals vs. plan are compared with variance explanations
- Leading indicators (not just lagging) are tracked

**5) ACTIONABILITY & DECISION SUPPORT**
- Asks and decisions needed are explicit
- Stakeholders know what action to take (if any)
- Reports enable informed decisions without additional meetings
- Follow-up on prior decisions/actions is tracked

**6) EFFICIENCY & AUTOMATION**
- Report creation is low-friction (templates, dashboards, automation)
- Data is pulled from systems (not manually compiled)
- Distribution is automated (scheduled emails, dashboards, portals)
- Time spent on reporting is proportional to value delivered

4) Quantify progress with metrics (% complete, velocity, burn rate) and show trends.
