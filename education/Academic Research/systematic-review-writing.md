# Systematic Review Writing

## Purpose
Create well-structured, clearly written, and methodologically rigorous systematic review manuscripts. This template guides the organization and writing of each review section from introduction through conclusions, emphasizing critical analysis, evidence-based argumentation, transparent reporting, and adherence to publication standards.

- Word Limit: [WORD_LIMIT]

### 4.2 Results Writing Guidelines

Writing principles:
- Objective presentation without interpretation
- Past tense for study results
- Clear organization with subheadings
- Heavy use of tables and figures
- Precise statistical reporting
- Complete reporting of all planned analyses

Statistical reporting:
- Effect sizes with confidence intervals
- P-values with statistical tests specified
- Heterogeneity statistics for meta-analyses
- Exact p-values when p < 0.05
- "p < 0.001" for very small values

Critical elements:
- [ ] PRISMA flow diagram included
- [ ] Study characteristics table provided
- [ ] Risk of bias assessment reported
- [ ] All planned analyses reported
- [ ] Figures appropriately referenced
- [ ] Statistical reporting complete
- [ ] GRADE assessment included

- Appropriate certainty level conveyed
- Clinical/practical relevance emphasized
- Future research clearly motivated

- Concise and focused
- Matches level of evidence

- Balances optimism with realism

- Overstating findings

- Ignoring limitations
- Vague recommendations

## Best Practices

1. **Follow reporting guidelines** - PRISMA, MOOSE, etc.
2. **Start with the results** - Know your findings before writing
3. **Outline before writing** - Create detailed structure
4. **Write for your audience** - Match journal's readership level
5. **Use clear, direct language** - Avoid unnecessary jargon
6. **Show, don't just tell** - Use tables and figures effectively
7. **Be transparent** - Acknowledge all limitations
8. **Match conclusions to evidence** - Don't overstate findings
9. **Get feedback early** - Share drafts with co-authors
10. **Follow journal guidelines** - Check author instructions carefully

## Tips for Success

- Write in multiple passes (structure → content → refinement)
- Start with easiest sections (often Methods and Results)
- Use consistent terminology throughout
- Create informative section headings
- Limit each paragraph to one main idea
- Use topic sentences effectively
- Include transition sentences between sections
- Cite appropriately (not too few or too many citations)
- Have non-experts read for clarity
- Allow time for multiple revisions (typically 4-8 weeks)
- Use journal's preferred reference style from the start
- Keep track of word counts by section
- Proofread carefully before submission
