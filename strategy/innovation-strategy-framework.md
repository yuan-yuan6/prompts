# Innovation Strategy Framework

## Purpose
Build systematic innovation capability by defining innovation ambition, developing portfolio across horizons, allocating resources effectively, and creating organizational culture that generates, selects, and scales breakthrough innovations aligned with strategic goals.

## Quick Start

**Want to build innovation strategy quickly?** Here's the minimal approach:

### When to Use This
- Need to systematize innovation beyond ad-hoc efforts
- Facing disruption and need innovation response
- Growth plateauing with existing products/services
- Building long-term competitive advantage through innovation
- Allocating innovation investments across portfolio

### Minimal Example
```
Company: Traditional retail bank
Challenge: Fintech disruption, digital transformation needed

Quick Innovation Strategy:
Horizon 1 (Core): Digital banking features (70% resources)
- Mobile app enhancements, AI customer service
- Timeline: 6-12 months, incremental revenue

Horizon 2 (Adjacent): Embedded finance (20% resources)
- Banking-as-a-service for e-commerce platforms
- Timeline: 12-24 months, new revenue streams

Horizon 3 (Transformational): Crypto/DeFi services (10% resources)
- Digital wallet, crypto trading, blockchain exploration
- Timeline: 24+ months, position for future

Innovation Processes:
- Stage-gate for H1 innovations
- Lean startup for H2/H3 experiments
- Dedicated innovation budget ($5M)
- Cross-functional innovation team

Result: Balanced portfolio protecting core while building future
```

### Basic 5-Step Workflow
1. **Define Innovation Ambition** - Where to play, how to win through innovation
2. **Build Innovation Portfolio** - Balance across 3 horizons
3. **Design Innovation Process** - How innovations move from idea to scale
4. **Allocate Resources** - Budget, talent, leadership attention
5. **Build Innovation Culture** - Mindsets, behaviors, incentives

**Time to complete**: 2-3 weeks for strategy, ongoing execution

**Pro tip**: Most companies over-invest in core (H1) and under-invest in future (H3) - deliberately balance

## PHASE 2: THREE HORIZONS PORTFOLIO

### 2.1 Horizon Framework

**Horizon 1: Extend & Defend Core (70-80% resources)**
- Focus: Incremental innovation in existing businesses
- Timeline: 0-12 months to revenue
- Risk: Low
- Return: Predictable
- Goal: Protect and grow core

**Horizon 2: Build Emerging Businesses (15-25% resources)**
- Focus: Adjacent innovations, new markets/products
- Timeline: 12-36 months to revenue
- Risk: Medium
- Return: High if successful
- Goal: Create next wave of growth

**Horizon 3: Create Viable Options (5-10% resources)**
- Focus: Breakthrough innovations, transformational
- Timeline: 36+ months to revenue
- Risk: High
- Return: Game-changing if successful
- Goal: Position for future disruption

## Best Practices

### Do's ✅
- **Balance Portfolio**: Don't starve future (H2/H3) to feed present (H1)
- **Kill Fast**: Terminate failing initiatives quickly to free resources
- **Celebrate Failures**: Learning from failure is part of innovation
- **Dedicate Resources**: Innovation needs protected budget and talent
- **Empower Teams**: Give autonomy with accountability
- **Connect to Strategy**: Innovation must serve strategic goals
- **Measure Rigorously**: Track inputs, process, outputs, and outcomes

### Don'ts ❌
- **Don't Just Brainstorm**: Need systematic process beyond ideation
- **Don't Only Incremental**: Balance with breakthrough innovation
- **Don't Ignore Culture**: Process without culture fails
- **Don't Over-Plan H3**: Accept uncertainty, emphasize learning
- **Don't Copy Other Companies**: Adapt innovation approach to your context
- **Don't Make Innovation Someone Else's Job**: Everyone's responsibility
