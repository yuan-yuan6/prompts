# Job Analysis & Role Definition

## Purpose
Define clear, comprehensive job requirements, competencies, and position descriptions that attract qualified candidates and set expectations for role success.

## Quick Start
1. **Gather Position Details** - Collect basic information: job title, department, reporting structure, and employment type
2. **Define Core Responsibilities** - List 5-7 primary duties that represent 70-80% of the role
3. **Specify Requirements** - Identify must-have qualifications (education, experience, skills) vs. nice-to-have attributes
4. **Set Success Metrics** - Define 3-5 measurable outcomes that indicate strong performance in the role
5. **Review and Refine** - Validate the job definition with hiring managers and ensure alignment with organizational needs

## Template

```
You are an expert in job analysis and role design. Create a detailed job definition and role framework based on the following information:

Position Context:
- Job Title: [POSITION_TITLE]
- Department: [DEPARTMENT_NAME]
- Reporting Structure: [REPORTING_RELATIONSHIP]
- Employment Type: [EMPLOYMENT_TYPE]
- Location Requirements: [LOCATION_FLEXIBILITY]
- Position Count: [NUMBER_OF_POSITIONS]

Organization Context:
- Company Name: [COMPANY_NAME]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY_TYPE]
- Company Size: [ORGANIZATION_SIZE]
- Growth Stage: [COMPANY_STAGE]

Role Details:
- Primary Responsibilities: [KEY_RESPONSIBILITIES]
- Required Qualifications: [MUST_HAVE_QUALIFICATIONS]
- Preferred Qualifications: [NICE_TO_HAVE_QUALIFICATIONS]
- Experience Level: [EXPERIENCE_REQUIREMENTS]
- Technical Skills: [TECHNICAL_COMPETENCIES]
- Soft Skills: [BEHAVIORAL_COMPETENCIES]
- Education Requirements: [EDUCATION_CRITERIA]
- Certifications: [CERTIFICATION_REQUIREMENTS]

Compensation Overview:
- Salary Range: [SALARY_BUDGET]
- Benefits Package: [BENEFITS_PACKAGE]
- Growth Opportunities: [DEVELOPMENT_OPPORTUNITIES]

Generate a comprehensive job analysis and role definition that includes:

## 1. COMPREHENSIVE JOB DESCRIPTION

### Position Overview
Provide a compelling summary including:
- Job title and level classification
- Department and team structure context
- Reporting relationships and hierarchy
- Strategic importance and organizational impact
- Growth and advancement trajectory
- Work environment and culture fit expectations

### Key Responsibilities

#### Primary Responsibilities (70-80% of role)
Detail core functions including:
- Daily, weekly, and monthly activities
- Key deliverables and outputs
- Performance expectations and standards
- Quality and productivity metrics
- Collaboration and teamwork requirements
- Customer or stakeholder interactions
- Project ownership and accountability

#### Secondary Responsibilities (20-30% of role)
Outline supporting activities:
- Cross-functional collaboration opportunities
- Special projects and strategic initiatives
- Mentoring and knowledge-sharing activities
- Process improvement contributions
- Innovation and continuous improvement expectations
- Backup and coverage responsibilities

### Success Metrics and KPIs
Define measurable outcomes:
- Performance measurement criteria (specific, quantifiable)
- Key result areas and objectives
- Quality standards and benchmarks
- Timeline and deadline expectations
- Budget and resource accountability
- Customer satisfaction metrics
- Team performance contributions

## 2. QUALIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

### Essential Qualifications
Specify must-have requirements:
- Education requirements (with acceptable alternatives)
- Minimum professional experience
- Industry-specific experience requirements
- Critical technical skill proficiencies
- Required certifications and licenses
- Language and communication capabilities
- Physical and environmental requirements
- Travel and schedule flexibility needs

### Preferred Qualifications
List nice-to-have attributes:
- Advanced education and specialized training
- Additional relevant experience
- Specialized skills and domain knowledge
- Industry recognition and achievements
- Leadership and management background
- Cross-functional or international experience
- Entrepreneurship or innovation track record

## 3. COMPETENCY FRAMEWORK

### Technical Competencies
Define job-specific capabilities:
- Core technical skills and proficiency levels
- Industry knowledge and expertise depth
- Software and system requirements
- Analytical and problem-solving abilities
- Process and methodology knowledge
- Quality and safety awareness
- Innovation and creativity expectations
- Continuous learning orientation

### Behavioral Competencies
Outline essential soft skills:
- Communication and interpersonal effectiveness
- Leadership and influence capabilities
- Teamwork and collaboration style
- Adaptability and resilience
- Initiative and self-motivation
- Customer focus and service orientation
- Ethical behavior and integrity
- Cultural awareness and sensitivity

## 4. ROLE ARCHITECTURE

### Day-in-the-Life Scenario
Create a realistic picture of typical workday activities, interactions, challenges, and wins.

### Career Progression Path
Outline potential advancement:
- Short-term development opportunities
- Medium-term career growth (1-3 years)
- Long-term advancement potential (3+ years)
- Skill development and training opportunities
- Cross-functional movement possibilities

### Team Integration
Describe collaborative context:
- Team structure and size
- Key internal partnerships and stakeholders
- Cross-functional collaboration requirements
- Communication patterns and expectations
- Decision-making authority and autonomy

## 5. WORK ENVIRONMENT

### Physical Environment
- Office, remote, or hybrid arrangement
- Workspace setup and equipment
- Environmental conditions
- Physical demands and requirements

### Schedule and Flexibility
- Standard working hours
- Flexibility options
- Peak periods and busy seasons
- On-call or after-hours expectations

### Company Culture Fit
- Values alignment requirements
- Cultural attributes and norms
- Work style preferences
- Team dynamics and collaboration style

Ensure the job analysis is:
- Clear and specific, avoiding generic language
- Balanced between aspirational and realistic
- Focused on essential requirements without unnecessary barriers
- Inclusive and accessible to diverse candidates
- Aligned with market standards and competitive positioning
- Actionable for both candidates and hiring teams
```

## Variables
- `[POSITION_TITLE]`: Specific job title
- `[DEPARTMENT_NAME]`: Department or team name
- `[REPORTING_RELATIONSHIP]`: Reports to (title/role)
- `[EMPLOYMENT_TYPE]`: Full-time, part-time, contract, etc.
- `[LOCATION_FLEXIBILITY]`: Remote, hybrid, on-site details
- `[NUMBER_OF_POSITIONS]`: How many openings
- `[COMPANY_NAME]`: Organization name
- `[INDUSTRY_TYPE]`: Industry sector
- `[ORGANIZATION_SIZE]`: Employee count or company size
- `[COMPANY_STAGE]`: Startup, growth, mature, etc.
- `[KEY_RESPONSIBILITIES]`: Main duties and activities
- `[MUST_HAVE_QUALIFICATIONS]`: Non-negotiable requirements
- `[NICE_TO_HAVE_QUALIFICATIONS]`: Desired but optional qualifications
- `[EXPERIENCE_REQUIREMENTS]`: Years and type of experience
- `[TECHNICAL_COMPETENCIES]`: Required technical skills
- `[BEHAVIORAL_COMPETENCIES]`: Required soft skills
- `[EDUCATION_CRITERIA]`: Degree requirements
- `[CERTIFICATION_REQUIREMENTS]`: Licenses or certifications needed
- `[SALARY_BUDGET]`: Compensation range
- `[BENEFITS_PACKAGE]`: Benefits summary
- `[DEVELOPMENT_OPPORTUNITIES]`: Growth and learning opportunities

## Usage Example
Use when defining a new role, updating job descriptions, establishing role requirements, creating competency frameworks, or preparing for recruitment.

## Customization Tips
- Use inclusive language that doesn't discourage diverse candidates
- Focus on outcomes and impact rather than just activities
- Distinguish between essential and preferred qualifications
- Provide specific examples of success metrics
- Consider work arrangement flexibility
- Align with industry standards while highlighting unique aspects
