## Purpose
Conduct comprehensive, systematic literature searches and synthesize existing research to build theoretical foundations, identify knowledge gaps, and establish the evidence base for new research investigations.

## 2. SEARCH STRATEGY DEVELOPMENT

### Database Selection
#### Primary Databases
- Discipline-specific databases:
  * PubMed/MEDLINE (health sciences)
  * ERIC (education)
  * PsycINFO (psychology)
  * Web of Science (multidisciplinary)
  * Scopus (multidisciplinary)
  * JSTOR (humanities, social sciences)
  * IEEE Xplore (engineering, computer science)
  * Business Source Complete (business)

#### Supplementary Sources
- Grey literature databases
- Dissertation and thesis repositories
- Government databases and reports
- Conference proceedings
- Clinical trial registries
- Preprint servers (arXiv, medRxiv, bioRxiv)
- Institutional repositories
- Specialized subject repositories

### Search Term Development
#### Keyword Identification
- Core concepts from research question
- Synonyms and related terms
- Alternative spellings and variations
- Acronyms and abbreviations
- Historical and contemporary terminology
- Discipline-specific language
- Controlled vocabulary (MeSH, subject headings)

#### Search String Construction
- Boolean operators (AND, OR, NOT):
  * AND: Narrows search (combines different concepts)
  * OR: Broadens search (synonyms, related terms)
  * NOT: Excludes terms (use cautiously)

- Phrase searching:
  * Use quotation marks for exact phrases
  * "systematic review" vs systematic review

- Truncation and wildcards:
  * * for multiple character variations (child* = child, children, childhood)
  * ? for single character variations (wom?n = woman, women)

- Proximity operators:
  * NEAR, ADJ, W/n for words near each other
  * Example: leadership NEAR/5 effectiveness

- Field-specific searching:
  * Title, abstract, keywords, author, year
  * TI("climate change") - search only in title

#### Example Search Strategy
```
(("research design*" OR methodology OR "research method*")
AND ("higher education" OR university OR college*)
AND (student* OR undergraduate*)
AND (engagement OR motivation OR persistence))
NOT ("K-12" OR secondary OR "high school")
```

### Search Filters and Limits
- Date range restrictions
- Publication type filters (articles, reviews, books)
- Language limitations
- Age group or population filters
- Study design filters (RCT, cohort, qualitative)
- Geographic filters
- Peer-review status
- Full-text availability

## 3. LITERATURE SEARCH EXECUTION

### Systematic Search Process
#### Database Searching
1. Run search in each selected database
2. Document search date and results count
3. Export citations to reference manager
4. Remove duplicates across databases
5. Track search iterations and modifications
6. Save search histories for reproducibility

#### Citation Management
- Reference management software:
  * Zotero (free, open-source)
  * Mendeley (free, collaborative)
  * EndNote (institutional, comprehensive)
  * RefWorks (cloud-based)

- Organization strategies:
  * Create project-specific libraries
  * Use folders and tags
  * Annotate and highlight PDFs
  * Link related articles
  * Track screening decisions

### Supplementary Search Methods
#### Citation Chaining
- Forward citation searching:
  * Identify newer articles citing key papers
  * Use Google Scholar "Cited by" feature
  * Track influence and impact

- Backward citation searching:
  * Review reference lists of key articles
  * Identify foundational studies
  * Follow theoretical lineage

#### Hand Searching
- Key journal table of contents
- Conference proceedings
- Organizational websites
- Expert blogs and newsletters
- Special issues on topic

#### Expert Consultation
- Contact authors in the field
- Request unpublished studies
- Identify ongoing research
- Verify search comprehensiveness
- Discover grey literature sources

## 4. SCREENING AND SELECTION

### Two-Stage Screening Process
#### Stage 1: Title and Abstract Screening
- Apply inclusion/exclusion criteria
- Independent screening by two reviewers
- Use screening software (Covidence, Rayyan, DistillerSR)
- Err on side of inclusion when uncertain
- Document reasons for exclusion
- Calculate inter-rater agreement (Cohen's kappa)
- Resolve disagreements through discussion

#### Stage 2: Full-Text Screening
- Retrieve full-text articles
- Apply detailed eligibility criteria
- Independent screening by two reviewers
- Document specific reasons for exclusion
- Track unavailable full-texts
- Contact authors if needed
- Final inclusion decisions

### Inclusion/Exclusion Criteria
#### Inclusion Criteria
- Relevant to research question
- Appropriate population or context
- Within specified time frame
- Acceptable study design
- Sufficient methodological quality
- Language accessibility
- Full-text available

#### Exclusion Criteria
- Off-topic or tangentially related
- Wrong population or setting
- Insufficient methodological rigor
- Duplicate publication
- Retracted articles
- Inaccessible full-text after attempts
- Language barriers

### PRISMA Flow Diagram
Document the screening process:
- Records identified through database searching
- Additional records from other sources
- Records after duplicates removed
- Records screened at title/abstract
- Records excluded with reasons
- Full-text articles assessed
- Full-text articles excluded with reasons
- Studies included in final review
- Studies included in meta-analysis (if applicable)

#### Types of Gaps
- Empirical gaps:
  * Unstudied populations

  * Lack of longitudinal studies

  * Conflicting theories
  * Missing mechanisms
  * Untested assumptions

- Practical gaps:
  * Implementation challenges
  * Intervention effectiveness
  * Real-world applicability
  * Policy implications unclear

## Best Practices

1. **Plan before searching** - Develop protocol with clear questions and criteria
2. **Search comprehensively** - Use multiple databases and supplementary methods
3. **Document everything** - Keep detailed records for reproducibility
4. **Screen systematically** - Use consistent criteria and independent reviewers
5. **Assess quality rigorously** - Apply appropriate critical appraisal tools
6. **Synthesize critically** - Analyze and interpret, don't just summarize
7. **Stay organized** - Use reference management software effectively
8. **Update regularly** - Set alerts for new publications on your topic
9. **Seek expertise** - Consult librarians and methodological experts
10. **Follow reporting standards** - Use PRISMA, ENTREQ, or other relevant guidelines
