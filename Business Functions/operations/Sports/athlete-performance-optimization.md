# Athlete Performance & Training Readiness Assessment

## Purpose
Assess readiness to **optimize athletic performance** through evidence-based training, recovery protocols, injury prevention, and performance monitoring. Use this to diagnose gaps in periodization, load management, and athlete support systems.

## Template

Conduct an athlete performance & training readiness assessment for {ATHLETE_CONTEXT}.

Score each dimension **1â€“5** (1 = ad hoc, 5 = optimized). Ground findings in observable signals (performance metrics, injury rates, recovery quality, training adaptation).

**1) TRAINING PERIODIZATION & PROGRAMMING**
- Annual plan follows periodization principles (prep, competition, transition phases)
- Training load progresses systematically (volume â†’ intensity â†’ specificity)
- Programs are individualized to athlete needs and response
- Microcycles balance stress and recovery (not constant grinding)

**2) PERFORMANCE MONITORING & DATA-DRIVEN ADJUSTMENT**
- Key performance indicators are tracked consistently (speed, power, endurance, skill)
- Load monitoring prevents overtraining (GPS, HRV, session RPE, wellness)
- Data informs training adjustments in near real-time (not retrospective only)
- Testing protocols are valid, reliable, and actionable

**3) RECOVERY & REGENERATION SYSTEMS**
- Sleep is prioritized and monitored (8+ hours, quality tracked)
- Nutrition supports training demands (periodized, hydration, supplements)
- Active recovery modalities are evidence-based (massage, compression, cryo, hydro)
- Recovery is individualized and load-responsive (not one-size-fits-all)

**4) INJURY PREVENTION & LOAD MANAGEMENT**
- Risk screening identifies vulnerable athletes (movement, history, workload)
- Prehabilitation is integrated into training (not bolted-on)
- Load spikes are managed (acute:chronic workload ratio monitored)
- Return-to-play protocols are progressive and criteria-based

**5) MENTAL SKILLS & PSYCHOLOGICAL READINESS**
- Mental skills training is integrated (goal-setting, visualization, self-talk, focus)
- Pressure and competition scenarios are practiced
- Mental health is monitored and supported (stress, burnout, anxiety)
- Confidence and resilience are developed systematically

**6) MULTIDISCIPLINARY TEAM & EXPERTISE**
- Coaches, sports scientists, physios, psychologists, nutritionists collaborate
- Evidence-based methods guide decisions (not just tradition or anecdote)
- Expertise gaps are filled (hiring, upskilling, partnerships)
- Athlete-centered approach prioritizes long-term development over short-term results
