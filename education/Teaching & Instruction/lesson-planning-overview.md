# Lesson Planning Overview

## Introduction
Effective lesson planning is the foundation of quality instruction, ensuring clear objectives, engaging activities, appropriate assessments, and differentiated support for all learners.

## Core Components

### 1. Objectives & Design
Define learning outcomes, align with standards, and design lesson structure.
→ **See**: [lesson-objectives-design.md](lesson-objectives-design.md)

### 2. Content & Activities
Develop instructional content, learning activities, and engagement strategies.
→ **See**: [lesson-content-activities.md](lesson-content-activities.md)

### 3. Assessment & Feedback
Create formative and summative assessments with effective feedback mechanisms.
→ **See**: [lesson-assessment-feedback.md](lesson-assessment-feedback.md)

### 4. Differentiation
Adapt instruction for diverse learners with varied needs and abilities.
→ **See**: [lesson-differentiation.md](lesson-differentiation.md)

### 5. Reflection & Improvement
Evaluate lesson effectiveness and implement continuous improvements.
→ **See**: [lesson-reflection-improvement.md](lesson-reflection-improvement.md)

## Planning Cycle
1. **Define Objectives**: Clear, measurable learning outcomes
2. **Design Activities**: Engaging, aligned instructional activities
3. **Create Assessments**: Valid, reliable measures of learning
4. **Differentiate**: Adapt for all learners
5. **Reflect**: Evaluate and improve continuously

## Best Practices
1. Start with clear learning objectives
2. Align activities to objectives
3. Use varied instructional strategies
4. Assess frequently and formatively
5. Differentiate for all learners
6. Reflect and adjust regularly
7. Engage students actively
8. Connect to prior knowledge
