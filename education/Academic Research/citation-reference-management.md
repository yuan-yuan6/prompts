## 3. CITATION STYLES AND FORMATTING

### 3.1 Major Citation Styles

#### APA Style (7th Edition)

In-text citation formats:
- Single author: (Smith, 2020)
- Two authors: (Smith & Jones, 2020)
- Three or more authors: (Smith et al., 2020)
- Multiple citations: (Jones, 2019; Smith, 2020)
- Direct quote: (Smith, 2020, p. 45)

Reference list format:
- Journal article:
  Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. Title of Journal, volume(issue), pages. https://doi.org/xxx

- Book:
  Author, A. A. (Year). Title of book (Edition). Publisher.

- Book chapter:
  Author, A. A. (Year). Title of chapter. In E. E. Editor (Ed.), Title of book (pp. xx-xx). Publisher.

Common in: Psychology, Education, Social Sciences

#### Vancouver Style (NLM)

In-text citation formats:
- Numbered citations: [1]
- Multiple citations: [1-3]
- Citation order: Numerical by first appearance

Reference list format:
- Journal article:
  Author AA, Author BB. Title of article. Journal Abbreviation. Year;volume(issue):pages.

- Book:
  Author AA, Author BB. Title of book. Edition. Place: Publisher; Year.

Common in: Medicine, Health Sciences, Biomedical Research

#### Harvard Style

In-text citation formats:
- Single author: (Smith 2020)
- Two authors: (Smith and Jones 2020)
- Three or more: (Smith et al. 2020)
- Page reference: (Smith 2020, p. 45)

Reference list format:
- Journal article:
  Author, A.A. and Author, B.B. (Year) 'Title of article', Journal Title, volume(issue), pp. pages.

- Book:
  Author, A.A. (Year) Title of book. Edition. Place: Publisher.

Common in: UK universities, Humanities, Social Sciences

#### Chicago Style

Notes and bibliography system:
- Footnote/endnote: [CHICAGO_NOTE_FORMAT]
- Bibliography: [CHICAGO_BIBLIOGRAPHY_FORMAT]

Author-date system:
- Similar to Harvard: [CHICAGO_AUTHOR_DATE]

Common in: History, Arts, Humanities

#### MLA Style (9th Edition)

In-text citation:
- Basic: (Author page)
- Example: (Smith 45)

Works Cited format:
- Author. "Title of Article." Title of Journal, vol. X, no. X, Year, pp. XX-XX.

Common in: Literature, Languages, Humanities

## 4. PLAGIARISM PREVENTION AND PROPER ATTRIBUTION

### 4.1 Understanding Plagiarism

#### Types of Plagiarism

Direct plagiarism:
- Definition: Copying text verbatim without quotation or citation
- Examples: [DIRECT_PLAGIARISM_EXAMPLES]
- Prevention: [DIRECT_PLAGIARISM_PREVENTION]
  * Use quotation marks for exact text
  * Always cite source
  * Limit direct quotations

Paraphrasing plagiarism:
- Definition: Restating ideas without citation
- Examples: [PARAPHRASE_PLAGIARISM_EXAMPLES]
- Prevention: [PARAPHRASE_PLAGIARISM_PREVENTION]
  * Substantially reword ideas
  * Change sentence structure
  * Always cite source of ideas

Mosaic/patchwork plagiarism:
- Definition: Combining copied phrases without quotation marks
- Examples: [MOSAIC_PLAGIARISM_EXAMPLES]
- Prevention: [MOSAIC_PLAGIARISM_PREVENTION]
  * Take notes in own words
  * Distinguish quotes from paraphrase
  * Cite all sources

Self-plagiarism:
- Definition: Reusing own previously published work
- Examples: [SELF_PLAGIARISM_EXAMPLES]
- Prevention: [SELF_PLAGIARISM_PREVENTION]
  * Cite own previous work
  * Disclose text reuse
  * Seek permission if needed

### 4.2 Proper Citation Practices

#### When to Cite

Always cite:
- Direct quotations
- Paraphrased ideas
- Statistics and data
- Theories and frameworks
- Others' research findings
- Visual materials (figures, tables)
- Computer code/algorithms

No citation needed:
- Common knowledge
- Your own original ideas
- Your own data/findings
- Generally known facts
- Proverbs and common sayings

#### Quotation Guidelines

Direct quotations:
Short quotes (< 40 words in APA):
- Format: "Text in quotation marks" (Author, Year, p. X)
- Example: [SHORT_QUOTE_EXAMPLE]

Long quotes (≥ 40 words in APA):
- Format: Block quotation without quotes
- Indentation: [BLOCK_QUOTE_INDENT]
- Citation placement: [BLOCK_QUOTE_CITATION]
- Example: [LONG_QUOTE_EXAMPLE]

Quote integration:
- Introducing quotes: [QUOTE_INTRODUCTION]
- Signal phrases: [SIGNAL_PHRASE_EXAMPLES]
  * "According to Smith (2020)..."
  * "As Jones argues..."
  * "Research shows that..."

#### Paraphrasing Best Practices

Effective paraphrasing:

2. Set aside and write from memory
3. Check against original

## Best Practices

1. **Choose software early** - Start using reference manager from day one
2. **Import systematically** - Establish consistent import procedures
3. **Verify metadata immediately** - Check accuracy upon import
4. **Organize as you go** - Don't wait until you have thousands of references
5. **Backup regularly** - Multiple backup locations
6. **Cite while writing** - Insert citations during drafting, not after
7. **Check formatting early** - Test bibliography formatting early in writing
8. **Maintain citation accuracy** - Regular audits throughout writing
9. **Understand plagiarism** - Know what requires citation
10. **Keep software updated** - Regular updates for compatibility

## Tips for Success

- Read your software's quick start guide and tutorials
- Use keyboard shortcuts for efficiency
- Create smart folders/collections for automatic organization
- Take advantage of browser connectors for one-click importing
- Attach PDFs to references for easy access
- Use notes fields for your own summaries and thoughts
- Export backup files regularly (at least weekly)
- Learn advanced search within your library
- Use groups/shared libraries for collaboration
- Check journal's author guidelines before finalizing formatting
- Allow time for citation troubleshooting (common with complex documents)
- Consider DOI over URL when both available (DOIs are permanent)
- Distinguish clearly between quotes and paraphrases in notes
- When in doubt about citation necessity, cite
- Keep original citation export files as backup
