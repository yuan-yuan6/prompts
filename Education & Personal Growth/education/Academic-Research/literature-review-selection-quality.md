# Literature Review Selection & Quality Assessment

## Purpose
Execute systematic study selection and screening processes, conduct comprehensive quality assessment and risk of bias evaluation, and document selection decisions transparently for rigorous literature reviews and evidence synthesis.

## Quick Start

### For Reviewers & Quality Assessors

**Step 1: Prepare Selection Infrastructure**
- Import search results into screening software (Covidence, Rayyan, etc.)
- Remove duplicate records across databases
- Assign reviewers to screening stages
- Establish consensus procedures for disagreements

**Step 2: Define Selection Criteria**
- Specify detailed inclusion criteria (PICO framework)
- Document exclusion criteria and rationale
- Create screening decision rules and examples
- Develop standardized exclusion reason categories

**Step 3: Execute Multi-Stage Screening (Ongoing)**
- Conduct title/abstract screening with dual review
- Perform full-text assessment of potentially relevant studies
- Document exclusion reasons at each stage
- Resolve disagreements through consensus or arbitration

**Step 4: Assess Study Quality (Variable)**
- Select appropriate quality assessment tools for study designs
- Conduct independent quality ratings by multiple reviewers
- Assess risk of bias across multiple domains
- Grade overall evidence quality and confidence

**Common Use Cases:**
- Systematic review study selection
- Meta-analysis eligibility screening
- Quality assessment for evidence grading
- Risk of bias evaluation
- PRISMA flow diagram creation

## Best Practices

1. **Conduct pilot screening before full review** - Test criteria and improve inter-rater reliability
2. **Use screening software for efficiency** - Tools like Covidence and Rayyan save significant time
3. **Maintain liberal inclusion at title/abstract stage** - "When in doubt, include" prevents premature exclusion
4. **Document all decisions transparently** - Complete audit trail enables reproducibility
5. **Calculate and report inter-rater reliability** - Demonstrates rigor and identifies training needs
6. **Match quality tools to study designs** - Different designs require different assessment approaches
7. **Conduct independent quality assessment** - Multiple raters prevent individual bias
8. **Integrate quality into synthesis** - Plan how quality will inform analysis and interpretation
9. **Create comprehensive PRISMA flow diagram** - Visual representation aids transparency
10. **Maintain excluded studies list with reasons** - Addresses potential reviewer questions

## Tips for Success

- Begin with calibration exercises to align reviewer understanding
- Establish regular team meetings to discuss challenging decisions
- Create clear decision rules for ambiguous situations with examples
- Use standardized data extraction forms for quality assessment
- Document rationale for all borderline inclusion/exclusion decisions
- Plan for author contact when eligibility information is missing
- Build buffer time for retrieval of hard-to-access full texts
- Consider quality threshold sensitivity analyses
- Use visualization tools for quality assessment results
- Integrate quality considerations into synthesis planning from the start
