## 1. THREE-ACT STRUCTURE

### Classical Structure Framework
#### Act One: Setup (Pages 1-30)
- Inciting incident and story catalyst
- Protagonist introduction and establishment
- World and rule establishment
- Conflict and opposition introduction
- Goal and objective clarification
- Stakes and consequence definition
- Theme and tone establishment
- Audience expectation setting

#### Act Two: Confrontation (Pages 30-90)
- Plot and character development progression
- Obstacle and challenge escalation
- Relationship and alliance formation
- Setback and failure integration
- Subplot and theme reinforcement
- Midpoint and direction change
- Character growth and transformation
- Tension and suspense building

#### Act Three: Resolution (Pages 90-120)
- Climax and confrontation
- Character arc completion
- Theme and message payoff
- Resolution and new equilibrium
- Loose end tying
- Emotional satisfaction
- Final image impact
- Audience fulfillment

### Plot Point Timing

- Act break and direction shift

- Goal and objective clarity
- Stakes elevation
- Audience hook solidification

##### Midpoint (Page 55-60)

- Direction clarification

##### Plot Point Two (Page 85-90)
