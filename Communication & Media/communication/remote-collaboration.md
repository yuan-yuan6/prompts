# Remote Collaboration & Distributed Team Readiness Assessment

## Purpose
Assess readiness to enable **effective remote collaboration** that maintains productivity, fosters connection, and manages complexity across distance and time zones. Use this to diagnose gaps in tools, workflows, communication norms, and team culture.

## Template

Conduct a remote collaboration & distributed team readiness assessment for {REMOTE_CONTEXT}.

Score each dimension **1â€“5** (1 = ad hoc, 5 = optimized). Ground findings in observable signals (productivity, engagement, communication effectiveness, tool adoption, team cohesion).

**1) TOOLS & TECHNOLOGY INFRASTRUCTURE**
- Collaboration tools are integrated and adopted (not disconnected silos)
- Video, chat, docs, and project tools work seamlessly
- Remote access is reliable and secure (VPN, cloud, mobile)
- Technical support is responsive and distributed-team-aware

**2) ASYNCHRONOUS WORKFLOWS & DOCUMENTATION**
- Async work is default (not synchronous bottlenecks)
- Decisions, context, and knowledge are documented (not in heads or Zoom recordings)
- Handoffs across time zones are smooth (clear ownership, no blocking)
- Status updates are written and searchable (not buried in meetings)

**3) COMMUNICATION NORMS & RESPONSIVENESS**
- Response time expectations are clear by channel (urgent vs. async)
- Overcommunication is valued (context, updates, transparency)
- Meeting norms exist (cameras on, agendas, recordings, time-zone-friendly)
- Digital body language is understood (no assumption of tone)

**4) TEAM CONNECTION & PSYCHOLOGICAL SAFETY**
- Intentional social connection happens regularly (not just work)
- Remote employees feel included and heard (not second-class)
- Trust is high (no micromanagement or surveillance culture)
- Serendipity is designed (virtual water cooler, informal channels)

**5) MANAGER CAPABILITY & REMOTE LEADERSHIP**
- Managers are trained on remote management (not just office skills)
- Output-focused (not presence-focused) performance management
- 1-on-1s are frequent and meaningful (not skipped or rushed)
- Coaching and feedback are effective remotely

**6) MEASUREMENT & CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT**
- Productivity and outcomes are tracked (not hours or activity)
- Engagement and connection are measured (surveys, retention, sentiment)
- Remote-specific challenges are identified and addressed
- Best practices are shared and scaled

## Example (Filled)

**Input**
- {REMOTE_CONTEXT}: 300-person software company. Fully remote (no office) since 2020. 12 time zones (US, Europe, Asia). Current engagement: 68% (goal 80%, down from 75% pre-pandemic). Productivity self-reported as "same or better" by 70%, but concerns about burnout (avg 50 hrs/week), silos (teams don't collaborate across geo), and new hire onboarding (6 months to full productivity, goal 3). Tools: Slack, Zoom, GitHub, Notion, Linear.
- {OBJECTIVES}: Increase engagement to 80%, reduce burnout (goal <45 hrs/week), improve cross-geo collaboration (goal 60% "collaborate effectively across teams"), accelerate onboarding (goal 3 months).
- {CONSTRAINTS}: No office (can't "just meet in person"); 12 time zones (no universal sync time); tool fatigue (resistance to adding more tools); managers promoted from IC (limited management training).

**1) EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**
- Overall maturity: 3.1 (Defined)
- Key risks: engagement declining, burnout rising, silos forming, onboarding slow
- Top improvements: (1) establish async-first workflows, (2) design intentional connection rituals, (3) train managers on remote leadership

**2) DIMENSION SCORECARD**

- Manager Capability: 2/5 â€” managers promoted from IC with no training; presence-focused (online hours); 1-on-1s sporadic

**3) TOOL & WORKFLOW AUDIT**

  - Zoom: 95% weekly usage, but "too many meetings" (avg 20 hrs/week in meetings)
  - GitHub: 90% daily (engineers), not used by non-tech roles

  - Linear: 85% adoption (product/eng), not used by other teams (silos)

  - Team celebrations: ad hoc (birthdays, launches), 50% participation

  - "I feel included in team decisions": 72% agree (goal 85%)
  - "Remote work doesn't disadvantage me": 68% agree (goal 90%)

## Best Practices (Exactly 8)
1) Default to async: document decisions, write updates, record meetingsâ€”don't assume everyone's in the room.
2) Overcommunicate context: remote work requires more explicit "why" and "how"â€”what's obvious to you isn't obvious to others.
3) Set clear response time expectations: urgent vs. async by channelâ€”remove ambiguity and reduce anxiety.
4) Design social connection intentionally: virtual coffee, celebrations, and offsites don't happen accidentallyâ€”schedule them.
5) Train managers on remote leadership: office management skills don't translateâ€”teach output-focus, async, and connection.
6) Measure outcomes, not activity: productivity is results delivered, not hours online or Slack messages sent.
7) Make meetings time-zone-friendly: rotate inconvenient times or record for async consumptionâ€”don't penalize geography.
8) Fight silos with documentation: tribal knowledge breeds silosâ€”write it down, make it searchable, and share widely.
