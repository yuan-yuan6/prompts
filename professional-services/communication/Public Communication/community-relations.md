## BEST PRACTICES

### Relationship Building
- Invest time in building genuine relationships before needing community support
- Listen actively to community concerns and respond with meaningful action
- Maintain consistent communication and follow through on commitments
- Respect community values, culture, and decision-making processes
- Offer assistance during community crises without expecting immediate returns
- Celebrate community successes and recognize local leaders and volunteers
- Be transparent about your organization's operations and community impact
- Engage authentically rather than just for positive publicity or marketing

### Program Development
- Base programs on genuine community needs rather than assumptions
- Include community members in program design and decision-making
- Measure success from the community's perspective, not just organizational metrics
- Build local capacity and sustainability rather than creating dependency
- Partner with existing community organizations rather than duplicating services
- Adapt programs based on community feedback and changing needs
- Document and share lessons learned to benefit other communities
- Plan for long-term commitment rather than short-term projects

### Stakeholder Engagement
- Map all stakeholders and understand their interests and influence levels
- Use appropriate communication channels for different stakeholder groups
- Provide multiple opportunities for community input and participation
- Address concerns promptly and transparently, even if you can't solve them immediately
- Recognize that community engagement takes time and patience
- Build coalitions around shared interests and common goals
- Respect the expertise and knowledge that community members bring
- Be prepared to adapt your approach based on stakeholder feedback and changing dynamics
