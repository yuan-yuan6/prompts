## Best Practices

1. **Start with clear objectives** - Define creative and business goals before conceptualizing
2. **Know your audience deeply** - Research demographics, preferences, and consumption patterns
3. **Create comprehensive mood boards** - Visual reference guides creative direction effectively
4. **Develop detailed storyboards** - Clear visual planning prevents production issues
5. **Test with animatics** - Validate timing and pacing before full production
6. **Maintain brand consistency** - Ensure all creative aligns with brand guidelines
7. **Document thoroughly** - Clear documentation facilitates smooth production handoff
8. **Iterate based on feedback** - Refine concepts through structured review cycles
9. **Consider technical feasibility** - Balance creative ambition with production capabilities
10. **Plan for accessibility** - Include inclusive design from the concept stage

## Tips for Success

- Begin with divergent thinking, then converge on the strongest concept
- Create multiple concept variations for client selection
- Use style frames to communicate visual direction clearly
- Include detailed timing notes in storyboards for accuracy
- Build flexibility into narrative structure for revision iterations
- Align creative decisions with measurable success metrics
- Involve key stakeholders early in concept development
- Document rationale behind creative decisions for reference
- Test concepts with representative audience members when possible
- Prepare for common client feedback and have alternatives ready
