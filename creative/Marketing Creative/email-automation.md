## 3. LEAD SCORING AND CUSTOMER LIFECYCLE MANAGEMENT
### 3.1 Lead Scoring Framework
#### Engagement and Behavior Scoring
##### Email Engagement Scoring
- Email open: 5 points
- Link click: 10 points
- Multiple clicks: 15 points
- Reply or forward: 20 points
- Social share: 15 points
- Unsubscribe: -50 points
- Spam complaint: -100 points
- Consistent engagement bonus

##### Digital Behavior Scoring
- Website visit and content consumption scoring
- Download and resource access weighting
- Event and webinar attendance value
- Social media engagement and sharing
- Survey response and feedback provision
- Support interaction and help seeking
- Purchase research and evaluation behavior
- Referral and advocacy actions

#### Demographic and Firmographic Scoring
##### Qualification Criteria Scoring
- Job title and decision-making authority
- Company size and revenue potential
- Industry and market segment relevance
- Geographic location and market priority
- Technology adoption and digital behavior
- Budget authority and purchasing power
- Timeline and urgency assessment
- Fit and qualification evaluation

##### Negative Scoring and Disqualification
- Non-target industry or segment
- Competitor or vendor identification
- Student or academic email domain
- Free email provider usage
- Invalid or bouncing email
- Unengaged or inactive status
- Explicit opt-out or disinterest
- Fraud or suspicious behavior

## IMPLEMENTATION GUIDANCE
### Automation Best Practices
- Start with simple workflows and add complexity gradually
- Map complete customer journeys before building automation
- Implement robust testing before launching workflows
- Monitor performance and optimize continuously
- Build in quality checks and manual review points
- Document workflows thoroughly for team reference
- Establish clear ownership and accountability
- Balance automation with personal touch

### Technical Considerations
- Ensure clean and accurate data for triggers
- Implement proper error handling and fallbacks
- Test across all workflow branches and scenarios
- Monitor automation performance and health
- Set up alerts for workflow failures or issues
- Maintain workflow versioning and change logs
- Plan for scale and increased volume
- Integrate with all necessary systems and data sources

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Over-automating and losing personalization
- Complex workflows that are difficult to maintain
- Insufficient testing leading to errors at scale
- Ignoring inactive or bouncing email addresses
- Not respecting unsubscribe and preferences
- Failing to update workflows with new learnings
- Poor segmentation leading to irrelevant messages
- Lack of coordination across multiple workflows

### Ensure the automation framework is
- Customer-journey focused and contextual
- Trigger-responsive and timely
- Personalized and relevant
- Well-tested and reliable
- Scalable and maintainable
- Performance-monitored and optimized
- Privacy-compliant and ethical
- Integrated with broader marketing systems
