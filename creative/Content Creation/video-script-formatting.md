## FORMATTING GUIDELINES

### Scene Headings
- Always UPPERCASE
- Include INT./EXT., LOCATION, TIME OF DAY
- Examples:
  * INT. OFFICE - DAY
  * EXT. PARK - NIGHT
  * INT./EXT. CAR (MOVING) - SUNSET

### Action Lines
- Present tense, active voice
- Only describe what can be SEEN or HEARD
- Keep paragraphs short (3-4 lines max)
- Introduce characters in UPPERCASE first time
- Use double-spacing between scenes

### Dialogue
- Character name centered, UPPERCASE
- Dialogue indented below character name
- Parentheticals for emotional tone (sparingly)
- (V.O.) for voice-over
- (O.S.) for off-screen dialogue

### Transitions
- RIGHT-aligned or separate line
- Examples: CUT TO:, DISSOLVE TO:, FADE OUT

### Camera Directions
- Use sparingly, only when essential
- Examples: CLOSE-UP, WIDE SHOT, PAN TO
- Describe the purpose, not the technical specs

## VISUAL STORYTELLING NOTATION

### Camera Movements
- PUSH IN: Slow dolly toward subject
- PULL BACK: Reveal wider context
- PAN: Horizontal camera movement
- TILT: Vertical camera movement
- TRACKING SHOT: Camera follows action
- CRANE: Elevated perspective change

### Visual Effects
- [SFX: Description of visual effect]
- [GRAPHIC: Text or image overlay]
- [TRANSITION: Specific effect name]
- [COLOR GRADE: Mood or style note]

### Timing Marks
- For commercials and tight formats:
  * :00-:05 Opening sequence
  * :05-:20 Main content
  * :20-:30 Call-to-action and close

## Best Practices

1. **Use industry-standard formatting** - Follow established conventions for readability
2. **Be visually specific** - Describe what the audience sees, not camera angles
3. **Keep dialogue natural** - Write how people actually speak
4. **Use white space effectively** - Don't crowd the page
5. **Number scenes consistently** - Help production track progress
6. **Include timing notes** - Especially for commercials and tight formats
7. **Mark revisions clearly** - Use color coding or version numbers
8. **Write for the ear** - Read dialogue aloud to test naturalness
9. **Be production-aware** - Consider feasibility of what you're scripting
10. **Stay format-consistent** - Don't switch formatting styles mid-script
