# Debt Management Readiness Assessment

## Purpose
Assess readiness to pay down debt across six dimensions: debt clarity, cashflow control, strategy selection, creditor optimization, behavior safeguards, and tracking/support.

## Template

Conduct a debt management readiness assessment for {DEBT_SNAPSHOT} with goals {PAYOFF_GOALS} and constraints {CONSTRAINTS}.

Score each dimension 1‚Äì5:

**1) DEBT INVENTORY & CLARITY**
- Complete list of debts (balance, APR, minimum, due date, status)
- Understanding of fees, penalties, and promotional rates
- Awareness of collections/legal risk if relevant

**2) CASHFLOW & BUDGET CONTROL**
- Stable plan for minimum payments (no missed payments)
- Budget identifies free cashflow for payoff
- Irregular expenses accounted for (prevents ‚Äúoops‚Äù months)

**3) PAYOFF STRATEGY FIT**
- Strategy chosen intentionally (avalanche vs snowball vs hybrid)
- Consolidation/refinance considered with clear pros/cons
- Plan includes emergency buffer to prevent re-borrowing

**4) CREDITOR OPTIMIZATION**
- Ability to negotiate APR/fees or request hardship plans (where appropriate)
- Understanding of balance transfer terms and risks
- Autopay and due-date alignment set

**5) BEHAVIOR & SAFEGUARDS**
- Spending triggers recognized (stress, convenience, social)
- Rules to prevent new debt (freeze cards, caps, alternate payment methods)
- Contingency plan for setbacks (income drop, surprise expense)

**6) TRACKING & SUPPORT**
- Weekly tracking of balances and payments
- Accountability mechanism (partner, coach, friend, spreadsheet/app)
- Progress celebration without self-sabotage

## Best Practices (exactly 8)

1) **Stabilize before optimizing** ‚Äî never miss minimum payments.
2) **Pick one payoff strategy** ‚Äî run avalanche/snowball for 90 days before changing.
3) **Build a small buffer** ‚Äî prevents re-borrowing during surprises.
4) **Reduce APR when possible** ‚Äî negotiation and refinancing can matter.
5) **Remove temptation** ‚Äî freeze cards and reduce easy access to credit.
6) **Track weekly, not daily** ‚Äî consistent visibility beats obsession.
7) **Plan for setbacks** ‚Äî define what you do when income drops.
8) **Celebrate milestones safely** ‚Äî reward progress without spending sprees.
