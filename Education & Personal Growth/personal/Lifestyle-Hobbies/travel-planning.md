# Travel Planning Readiness Assessment

## Purpose
Assess readiness to plan a trip effectively across six dimensions: clarity, budget/booking, research/risk, itinerary design, logistics/tools, and flexibility/wellbeing. This produces a plan and timeline that reduces surprises.

## Template

Conduct a travel planning readiness assessment for {TRIP} with priorities {PRIORITIES} and constraints {CONSTRAINTS}.

Score each dimension 1‚Äì5:

**1) CLARITY & DECISION READINESS**
- Destination/dates/duration are locked (or a decision method exists)
- Traveler preferences and non-negotiables are explicit
- Tradeoffs accepted (budget vs comfort, pace vs depth, cities vs nature)

**2) BUDGET & BOOKING READINESS**
- Realistic total budget and allocation (lodging/transport/food/activities/buffer)
- Booking windows understood (flights, lodging, timed-entry activities)
- Payment and cancellation strategy (refundable vs non-refundable)

**3) DESTINATION RESEARCH & RISK**
- Entry requirements (passport validity, visas, vaccinations) understood
- Safety, scams, and neighborhood considerations researched
- Insurance and contingency thinking (medical, cancellations, disruptions)

**4) ITINERARY DESIGN (PACE + GEOGRAPHY)**
- Geographic clustering to reduce transit friction
- Daily structure that avoids over-scheduling (one anchor + flexible blocks)
- Rest and recovery planned (especially for kids/jet lag)

**5) LOGISTICS & TOOLS**
- Transportation plan (airport transfers, local transit, car needs)
- Reservation organization (confirmations, offline access, shared docs)
- Packing/gear readiness aligned to weather and activities

**6) FLEXIBILITY & WELLBEING**
- ‚ÄúMust-do‚Äù vs ‚Äúnice-to-do‚Äù is separated
- Backup plans exist for weather, closures, low energy
- Communication and expectations aligned among travelers

- Flights/transport, lodging, activities, food, documentation, safety

## Best Practices (exactly 8)

1) **Decide the trip ‚Äúshape‚Äù first** ‚Äî pace and base matter more than attractions.
2) **Use one anchor per day** ‚Äî everything else stays flexible.
3) **Cluster geographically** ‚Äî reduce transit to increase enjoyment.
4) **Budget with a buffer** ‚Äî plan categories + 10‚Äì15% contingency.
5) **Book in the right order** ‚Äî flights/lodging first, then timed-entry, then restaurants.
6) **Organize confirmations offline** ‚Äî assume you‚Äôll lose connectivity at the worst time.
7) **Plan for constraints explicitly** ‚Äî mobility, kids, jet lag, diet, weather.
8) **Create two backup days** ‚Äî indoor options and low-effort alternates prevent spiral.
