## Template

Design {ASSET_TYPE} for {CLIENT} serving {AUDIENCE} expressing {BRAND_PERSONALITY} across {PLATFORMS}.

**BRAND STRATEGY AND VISUAL IDENTITY**

Define brand personality translating abstract values into concrete visual decisions. Professional trustworthy brands use conservative colors (blues, grays), generous whitespace, serif or geometric san-serif typography creating calm authority suitable for financial services, healthcare, legal practices. Energetic friendly brands employ vibrant colors (oranges, greens, purples), playful rounded typography, asymmetric layouts creating approachable enthusiasm appropriate for consumer products, fitness brands, children's services. Technical precise brands feature monochromatic palettes, grid-based layouts, monospace or grotesque fonts communicating competence for software companies, engineering firms, data platforms.

Develop color psychology strategy matching emotional goals and cultural context. Red communicates energy, passion, urgency (restaurants, entertainment, sales) but signals danger or debt in financial contexts. Blue conveys trust, stability, professionalism (technology, finance, healthcare) but appears cold or corporate avoiding consumer lifestyle brands. Green represents growth, sustainability, health (environmental organizations, wellness products) but lacks premium positioning for luxury goods. Purple suggests creativity, luxury, spirituality (beauty brands, premium products) but appears juvenile in serious business contexts.

Establish competitive differentiation through visual positioning. Audit 5-7 direct competitors documenting color palettes, typography choices, design styles identifying saturation and gaps. Financial services sector saturated with blue-gray schemes creating differentiation opportunity through warm earth tones maintaining professionalism. Tech startup space crowded with geometric sans-serifs and gradient logos suggesting differentiation through friendly hand-drawn elements or bold serifs. Healthcare industry dominated by light blues and sterile aesthetics enabling differentiation through warm approachable colors maintaining trust signals.

Create logo types matching brand strategy and application needs. Wordmark logos use customized typography alone (Google, Coca-Cola, FedEx) requiring distinctive type treatment, working well when brand name short and memorable, scaling excellently across sizes. Icon-based logos use symbolic representation (Apple, Nike, Twitter) enabling small-scale recognition, transcending language barriers, requiring strong simple concept. Combination marks integrate both (Adidas, Burger King, Lacoste) providing flexibility using full version or icon alone, balancing recognition and identification.

**TYPOGRAPHY SYSTEM AND HIERARCHY**

Select typeface families balancing aesthetics with functionality. Primary typeface defines brand personality: serif fonts (Times, Garamond, Playfair) convey tradition and authority, sans-serif (Helvetica, Futura, Montserrat) communicate modernity and clarity, script fonts (Pacifico, Dancing Script) suggest elegance or casualness, display fonts (Impact, Bebas Neue) demand attention but require restraint. Limit to 2-3 typefaces maximum preventing visual chaos: primary for headlines and branding, secondary for body copy, optional display for special emphasis.

Define type scale using consistent ratio creating hierarchical clarity. Use 1.25 ratio (major third): 12px, 15px, 19px, 24px, 30px, 38px, 48px, 60px providing sufficient differentiation while maintaining harmony. Map to semantic uses: Display 48-60px (hero headlines), H1 38-48px (page titles), H2 30px (major sections), H3 24px (subsections), Body 16-19px (readable paragraph text), Caption 12-15px (metadata, footnotes). Print typically uses larger sizes: Body 10-12pt, Headlines 24-72pt accounting for reading distance.

Specify line-height, letter-spacing, and text treatments ensuring readability. Body text uses 1.5-1.6 line-height preventing cramped appearance: 16px text with 24-26px line-height creates comfortable reading rhythm. Headlines use tighter 1.1-1.3 line-height emphasizing impact over comfort. Increase letter-spacing (tracking) 50-100 units on all-caps text improving legibility, decrease slightly -20 to -50 on large display type achieving optical balance. Limit line length 60-80 characters optimal for reading: longer causes eye fatigue and line-skipping, shorter feels choppy.

Implement accessibility ensuring inclusive typography. Minimum 16px (12pt) body text for comfortable reading by older adults or low-vision users. Maintain 4.5:1 contrast ratio between text and background meeting WCAG AA standards: black text (#000000) on white (#FFFFFF) achieves 21:1, dark gray (#333333) on white achieves 12.6:1. Avoid pure red-green color coding failing colorblind users: supplement color with icons, patterns, or text labels. Support dyslexic readers through generous line-spacing, left-aligned text (not justified), sans-serif fonts designed for legibility (OpenDyslexic, Lexie Readable).

**COLOR SYSTEM AND PALETTE DEVELOPMENT**

Design primary color with multiple shade variations supporting diverse applications. Generate 9-value scale from single base color: 50 (lightest tint for backgrounds), 100-400 (progressively darker backgrounds and borders), 500 (base brand color for logos and primary buttons), 600-800 (hover states and secondary uses), 900 (darkest shade for text on light backgrounds). Example: Brand blue #0066FF (base 500) generates 100 #E6F0FF (subtle background), 700 #0052CC (button hover), 900 #003D99 (body text).

Create complementary palette balancing unity with variety. Analogous colors (adjacent on color wheel) create harmony: blue primary with cyan and purple accents maintaining cohesive calm aesthetic. Complementary colors (opposite on wheel) create dynamic tension: orange brand with blue accents providing energetic contrast. Split-complementary (base plus two adjacent to complement) offers vibrant balance without full complementary intensity: red primary with yellow-green and blue-green accents. Triadic (three equally-spaced) provides bold balanced palette: red, yellow, blue primary palette creating vibrant playful identity.

Specify color for print versus digital accounting for reproduction differences. Print uses CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Black) subtractive color: vibrant screen blues often dull in CMYK requiring adjustment, rich blacks use C:60 M:40 Y:40 K:100 versus flat K:100 achieving depth. Digital uses RGB (Red, Green, Blue) additive color offering brighter saturation especially greens and blues. Pantone spot colors ensure print consistency specifying exact ink formulations: Pantone 294 C (Yale blue) reproduces identically across printers versus CMYK approximations varying by press and paper.

Document color values comprehensively enabling consistent reproduction. Specify all formats: HEX #0066FF (web), RGB (0, 102, 255) digital, CMYK (100, 60, 0, 0) print, Pantone 2935 C (spot color). Note accessibility: primary blue passes WCAG AA on white (5.9:1 contrast) but fails on light gray requiring darker shade. Define usage rules: primary color 40% maximum page coverage preventing overwhelming, neutral grays 50-60% creating balance, accent colors 10% maximum providing emphasis without chaos.

**LAYOUT DESIGN AND COMPOSITION PRINCIPLES**

Implement grid systems providing structure and flexibility. 12-column grid divides cleanly into halves (2Ã—6), thirds (3Ã—4), quarters (4Ã—3) enabling diverse layouts: full-width hero (12 columns), two-column article (6-6), sidebar layout (3-9 or 4-8). Define gutters (spacing between columns) 20-30px maintaining breathing room. Magazine layouts often use baseline grids aligning text across columns: 6pt baseline grid with 12pt line-height creates vertical rhythm.

Apply composition principles creating visual interest and balance. Rule of thirds divides canvas into 9 equal parts positioning focal points at intersections creating dynamic asymmetry versus static centering. Golden ratio (1:1.618) creates aesthetically pleasing proportions: rectangle 800px Ã— 494px, layout margins 62% primary content 38% sidebar. Visual weight balances elements: large light object counterbalances small dark object, text block balances image, negative space balances dense content preventing overcrowding.

Design information hierarchy guiding viewer attention through priority. Primary elements use size, position, contrast commanding attention: large bold headline top-center, contrasting color call-to-action button. Secondary elements support without competing: medium subheadings, standard body text, subtle background imagery. Tertiary elements provide detail on demand: small captions, footnotes, supplementary links. Create visual flow following F-pattern (western reading: top-left to right, down left side) or Z-pattern (marketing: top-left to top-right, diagonal to bottom-left, across to bottom-right).

Utilize whitespace (negative space) preventing cluttered overwhelming layouts. Micro whitespace between letters (tracking), lines (leading), and UI elements improves readability and sophistication: generous 40-60px padding around cards, 20-30px between paragraphs. Macro whitespace separates major sections creating breathing room and emphasis: 80-120px vertical spacing between homepage sections, asymmetric layouts leaving 40-50% canvas empty. Luxury brands maximize whitespace creating premium perception, budget brands minimize maximizing information density.

**LOGO DESIGN AND BRAND MARK DEVELOPMENT**

Develop logo concept translating brand essence into memorable symbol. Abstract geometric logos suggest modernity and scalability (Mastercard circles, Microsoft squares) working across cultures without literal interpretation. Literal pictorial logos represent products or services directly (Apple, Shell, Starbucks mermaid) providing immediate recognition trading cultural universality for specific association. Letter-based monograms (IBM, HBO, CNN) suit long brand names requiring abbreviation, creating proprietary letterforms.

Construct logo using geometric precision ensuring reproduction consistency. Build on foundational grid establishing proportions and relationships: circular logo fits perfect circle, height equals width, internal elements align to grid intersections. Specify construction lines, angles, curves enabling exact recreation: "O" uses 60-unit circle, stems 10 units wide, crossbar positioned at 55% height. Define minimum size maintaining legibility: 24px digital (legible on mobile), 0.5 inch print (business card clarity). Test reductions ensuring details survive: eliminate fine details below minimum size, provide simplified version for small applications.

Create logo variations supporting diverse contexts. Primary full-color version represents ideal application on white or light backgrounds. Reversed version works on dark backgrounds or photographs maintaining visibility. Single-color versions enable limited-color printing: black for newspaper, white for signage, primary brand color for promotional items. Horizontal and vertical orientations accommodate different layouts: horizontal for letterhead headers, vertical (stacked) for social media avatars. Simplified versions for favicons and app icons: 16Ã—16px to 512Ã—512px maintaining recognizability without detail.

Design logo applications demonstrating versatility across touchpoints. Business cards show logo at small scale with contact information maintaining professionalism. Website header displays logo prominently establishing brand presence while allowing navigation. Product packaging integrates logo with legal requirements and product information. Vehicle graphics adapt logo to long horizontal format maximizing visibility. Specify clear space (minimum margin around logo equal to height of logo element) preventing crowding by other design elements protecting brand integrity.

**INFOGRAPHIC AND DATA VISUALIZATION DESIGN**

Structure information flow telling coherent data story. Start with headline summarizing key insight: "Global Temperature Increased 1.1Â°C Since 1880" versus generic "Climate Data." Organize content logically: temporal data uses timeline, comparative data uses side-by-side charts, hierarchical data uses tree diagrams, relational data uses network maps. Progressive disclosure reveals complexity incrementally: overview statistics â†’ detailed breakdown â†’ methodology notes preventing initial overwhelm.

Select chart types matching data relationships and audience sophistication. Bar charts compare discrete categories (sales by region, survey responses) using length encoding highly accurate human perception. Line graphs show trends over time (stock prices, website traffic) connecting data points revealing patterns. Pie charts display parts-of-whole (budget allocation, market share) limited to 5-6 segments before becoming unreadable. Scatter plots reveal correlations (income vs. happiness, ad spend vs. conversions) showing relationships between variables.

Design visual encoding ensuring accuracy and honesty. Maintain proportional representation: doubling data value doubles visual element size, Y-axis starts at zero preventing exaggerated differences. Avoid 3D effects and decorative distortion creating misleading perspective: 3D pie charts misrepresent slice sizes through angle distortion. Label directly rather than relying solely on color: add text annotations, use patterns plus color, provide legends when direct labeling impractical. Show uncertainty and limitations: error bars on scientific data, sample sizes on survey results, data source citations establishing credibility.

Integrate brand identity maintaining recognition without overwhelming data. Apply brand colors to charts using primary for main data, accent for highlights, neutrals for context: brand blue for revenue line, gray for industry benchmark, red accent for forecast. Use brand typography with careful sizing: smaller text in charts versus marketing materials ensuring legibility at data densities. Add subtle logo watermark or branded footer without competing with information. Balance aesthetic polish with functional clarity: beautiful charts that communicate accurately better than either alone.

**PRINT PRODUCTION AND TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS**

Define print specifications ensuring quality reproduction. Resolution 300 DPI minimum for photos and raster graphics: 8Ã—10 inch photo requires 2400Ã—3000 pixels, lower resolution appears pixelated when printed. Vector graphics (logos, type) scale infinitely but embed fonts or convert to outlines preventing font substitution. Bleed extends design 0.125 inch (3mm) beyond trim ensuring edge-to-edge coverage accounting for cutting variance. Safe area maintains critical content 0.25 inch (6mm) inside trim preventing accidental cropping.

Specify color mode and profiles for consistent output. CMYK color mode for offset printing using cyan, magenta, yellow, black inks mixing to create colors: convert RGB files to CMYK before printing preventing unexpected color shifts. Pantone spot colors specify exact ink formulas bypassing CMYK approximations: brand blue Pantone 2935 C reproduces identically across printers. Color profiles (ISO Coated v2 common standard) ensure cross-device consistency calibrating for specific printing conditions. Request printed proof before full run verifying colors, checking typos, confirming layout.

Choose paper stocks balancing aesthetics, functionality, and budget. Text weight 60-100# suitable for flyers, letterhead, inserts providing economical flexibility. Cover weight 80-130# for business cards, postcards, brochure covers creating substantial premium feel. Coatings affect appearance and durability: matte creates sophisticated understated look, gloss enhances colors and vibrancy, uncoated absorbs ink creating organic tactile quality. Specialty stocks (metallic, textured, recycled) differentiate brand but cost 2-5x standard.

Prepare files for professional printing preventing costly errors. Collect fonts embedding or outlining preventing substitution when printer lacks typeface. Package linked images confirming all graphics included at correct resolution. Check overprint and transparency flattening ensuring layered effects reproduce correctly. Export to print-ready PDF/X-1a standard embedding everything printer needs. Include bleed marks, crop marks, color bars enabling accurate trimming and color calibration.

Deliver graphic design strategy as:

1. **BRAND IDENTITY SYSTEM** - Logo variations, color palette with values, typography specifications, usage guidelines

2. **DESIGN TEMPLATES** - Layouts for key applications (business cards, letterhead, presentations, social media, website)

3. **STYLE GUIDE** - Comprehensive brand standards documenting all visual elements and usage rules

4. **PRODUCTION FILES** - Print-ready PDFs, web-optimized PNGs, editable source files (AI/PSD/INDD)

5. **ASSET LIBRARY** - Organized collection of logos, icons, patterns, photography, illustrations

6. **TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS** - Print specs (CMYK, Pantone, paper stocks), digital specs (color profiles, file formats, dimensions)
