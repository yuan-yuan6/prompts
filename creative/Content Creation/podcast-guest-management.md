## GUEST OUTREACH TEMPLATES

### Initial Contact Email
```
Subject: Invitation to [Podcast Name] - [Topic]

Hi [Guest Name],

I'm [Your Name], host of [Podcast Name], a [format] podcast focused on [niche/topic].

I've been following your work on [specific achievement/project], and was particularly impressed by [specific detail]. I think my audience of [audience description] would find tremendous value in your insights on [topic].

I'd love to have you as a guest to discuss [specific topic/angle]. Episodes are typically [duration] and can be recorded at your convenience via [platform].

Some topics I'd love to explore with you:
- [Topic 1]
- [Topic 2]
- [Topic 3]

Would you be interested in joining me for a conversation? I'm flexible on timing and happy to work around your schedule.

Looking forward to hearing from you!

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Podcast Name]
[Website/Social Links]
```

### Follow-Up Email (1 week later)
```
Subject: Re: Invitation to [Podcast Name]

Hi [Guest Name],

I wanted to follow up on my invitation from last week to join [Podcast Name] as a guest.

I understand you're busy, so I wanted to make this as easy as possible:
- Recording is just [duration], done remotely
- You choose the date/time that works for you
- We handle all editing and promotion
- You'll get a copy to review before publishing

Recent guests include [notable guest names], and episodes typically reach [audience size/metrics].

If you're interested, simply reply with your general availability and I'll send over some time options.

If now isn't the right time, no worries at all - perhaps we can connect in the future.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

Hi [Guest Name],

Great! I'm excited to have you on [Podcast Name].

Here are the details:
- **Date**: [Day, Month Date, Year]

Looking forward to our conversation,
[Your Name]

P.S. Where can I find your bio, headshot, and any links you'd like included in the show notes?

- [ ] Send confirmation email with details

- [ ] Share discussion topics and questions

### 1-2 Days Before
- [ ] Send reminder email with join link
- [ ] Confirm technical setup complete

- [ ] Research any recent updates
- [ ] Prepare backup questions
- [ ] Test recording equipment

### Day Of
- [ ] Send friendly reminder message
- [ ] Set up recording environment
- [ ] Test all equipment and software
- [ ] Prepare notes and questions
- [ ] Have water and supplies ready
- [ ] Start recording 15 min early for tech check

## INTERVIEW DAY WORKFLOW

### Pre-Recording (15 minutes)
1. **Tech Check**
   - Audio levels and quality
   - Video setup (if applicable)
   - Internet connection stability
   - Recording software functioning
   - Backup systems ready

2. **Rapport Building**
   - Casual conversation
   - Make guest comfortable
   - Discuss format and flow
   - Answer any questions
   - Set expectations

3. **Final Preparation**
   - Review key topics
   - Confirm sensitive areas
   - Discuss time limits
   - Start recording

### During Recording
1. **Opening**
   - Start with energy
   - Introduce guest warmly
   - Establish context
   - Set up first topic

2. **Main Content**
   - Follow question flow
   - Practice active listening
   - Ask follow-up questions
   - Manage time and pacing
   - Note great moments for clips

3. **Closing**
   - Summarize key points
   - Thank guest sincerely
   - Get their call-to-action
   - Ask for contact info to share

### Post-Recording (10 minutes)
1. **Debrief**
   - Thank them again
   - Discuss highlights
   - Confirm next steps
   - Ask for feedback

2. **Logistics**
   - Confirm bio and links
   - Explain editing process
   - Set review timeline
   - Discuss promotion plan

## DIFFICULT SITUATIONS GUIDE

### Nervous Guest
- Start with easy questions
- Pause recording to reassure
- Share that editing can fix anything
- Focus on conversation, not performance

### Overly Promotional Guest
- Redirect to value and stories
- Ask: "How does that benefit listeners?"
- Limit promotional time to end
- Edit out excessive plugs

### Disagreement on Topic
- Respectfully explore both views
- Focus on understanding, not convincing
- Find common ground
- Agree to disagree professionally

### Technical Difficulties
- Have phone backup option
- Record locally as backup
- Stay calm and patient
- Reschedule if necessary

## Best Practices

1. **Make it easy** - Reduce friction at every step for guests
2. **Over-communicate** - Provide clear expectations and instructions
3. **Be professional** - Respect their time and expertise
4. **Show appreciation** - Thank guests genuinely and publicly
5. **Follow through** - Do what you say you'll do
6. **Build relationships** - Think long-term, not transactional
7. **Add value** - Help promote guests and their work
8. **Stay organized** - Track all interactions and commitments
9. **Be flexible** - Accommodate guest needs when possible
10. **Maintain quality** - Ensure great guest experience every time

## Tips for Success

- Start outreach 4-6 weeks before desired recording date
- Send calendar invites with all details immediately upon booking
- Create a guest information packet with technical setup guides
- Record and share sample clips for guest promotion
- Build a "dream guest" list and chip away at it
- Leverage existing guests for introductions to new guests
- Create templates for all communications to save time
- Follow up with guests 6 months later for updates
- Celebrate guest milestones and achievements publicly
- Turn great guests into recurring contributors
