## Template

Conduct a vendor management & procurement readiness assessment for {VENDOR_PORTFOLIO}.

Score each dimension **1â€“5** (1 = ad hoc, 5 = optimized). Provide evidence-based findings and actionable recommendations.

**1) VENDOR SEGMENTATION & STRATEGY**
- Vendors are segmented by criticality, spend, and strategic value
- Sourcing strategy exists per category (single vs. multi-source, TCO-based)
- Vendor selection criteria are clear and consistently applied
- Portfolio is balanced for risk, innovation access, and cost optimization

**2) CONTRACT & RELATIONSHIP MANAGEMENT**
- Contracts are clear on scope, pricing, SLAs, termination, and risk allocation
- Renewal pipeline and negotiation strategy are proactive and data-driven
- Governance structure defines decision rights, cadence, and escalation paths
- Strategic vendors have engagement models that foster partnership and co-innovation

**3) PERFORMANCE MEASUREMENT & ACCOUNTABILITY**
- KPIs are defined per vendor and tied to business outcomes
- Scorecards track quality, delivery, cost, responsiveness, and compliance
- Performance reviews happen on schedule with documented action plans
- Recognition and consequences exist for high/low performers

**4) RISK, COMPLIANCE & CONTINUITY**
- Vendor risk is assessed (financial, operational, cyber, ESG, concentration)
- Critical vendors have business continuity and disaster recovery plans
- Compliance and audit requirements are tracked and enforced
- Incident response and escalation protocols are tested and documented

**5) OPERATIONAL EFFICIENCY & SYSTEMS**
- Procurement processes are standardized and automated where appropriate
- Purchase-to-pay workflows minimize cycle time and approval friction
- Spend analytics and reporting provide visibility by category, vendor, and trend
- Vendor portals or systems enable self-service and transparency

**6) CAPABILITY & VENDOR DEVELOPMENT**
- Procurement team has skills in negotiation, category management, and risk
- Vendor training and onboarding programs exist for complex engagements
- Collaboration forums drive innovation and continuous improvement
- Vendor exit and transition processes minimize disruption

## Best Practices (Exactly 8)
1) Segment vendors by criticality and spend; tailor governance and KPIs accordingly.
2) Define SLAs explicitly in contracts with measurement, reporting, and consequences.
3) Track performance with scorecards reviewed quarterly for strategic vendors.
4) Assess and mitigate concentration risk; maintain backup options for critical services.
5) Validate business continuity plans annually with tabletop exercises.
6) Automate spend analytics and contract lifecycle tracking to reduce manual overhead.
7) Build strategic partnerships through joint innovation forums and value-sharing.
8) Establish exit and transition playbooks to minimize disruption when switching vendors.
