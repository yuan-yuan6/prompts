# Press Release & Media Relations Readiness Assessment

## Purpose
Assess readiness to **generate earned media coverage, manage journalist relationships, and control narrative** through effective press releases, media outreach, and spokesperson performance. Use this to diagnose gaps in news judgment, media targeting, and message discipline.

## Template

Conduct a press release & media relations readiness assessment for {MEDIA_CONTEXT}.

Score each dimension **1â€“5** (1 = ad hoc, 5 = optimized). Ground findings in observable signals (media coverage volume/tone, journalist relationships, message consistency, spokesperson performance).

**1) NEWS JUDGMENT & STORY DEVELOPMENT**
- Newsworthy stories are identified proactively (not just announcements)
- Angles are tailored to journalist beats and audience interests
- Timing is strategic (industry events, news cycles, exclusives)
- Non-newsworthy items are handled through owned channels (not press releases)

**2) PRESS RELEASE QUALITY & AP STYLE**
- Headlines are compelling and concise (8â€“10 words, active voice)
- Lead paragraphs answer who/what/when/where/why in 1â€“2 sentences
- Quotes add color and humanity (not bland corporate speak)
- Boilerplate is current, concise, and relevant

**3) MEDIA TARGETING & JOURNALIST RELATIONSHIPS**
- Media lists are segmented by beat, outlet type, and audience
- Journalists are researched (recent articles, interests, preferences)
- Relationships are cultivated consistently (not just during pitches)
- Pitches are personalized and concise (why this matters to their audience)

**4) SPOKESPERSON READINESS & MESSAGE DISCIPLINE**
- Spokespeople are trained and media-ready (message delivery, bridging, tough questions)
- Key messages are clear, consistent, and memorable
- Answers stay on-message while feeling authentic (not robotic)
- Crisis scenarios are practiced and prepared

**5) DISTRIBUTION & FOLLOW-UP EXECUTION**
- Distribution timing and channels are optimized (embargo strategy, wire services)
- Pitches are sent strategically (exclusives to tier-1, then broader)
- Follow-up is persistent but respectful (no spam, add value)
- Multimedia assets are professional and accessible (photos, videos, data)

**6) MEASUREMENT & CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT**
- Media coverage is tracked (volume, tone, key messages, reach, share-of-voice)
- Journalist response rates and conversion are measured
- Post-mortems identify what worked and what to improve
- Media strategy evolves based on performance data

## Best Practices (Exactly 8)

2) Lead with the angle, not the announcement: "Cybersecurity breaches up 40%â€”here's why" beats "Company X Launches Product Y."
3) Personalize every pitch: reference journalist's recent work and explain why your story fits their beat.
4) Train spokespeople relentlessly: media skills aren't innateâ€”practice message discipline, bridging, and tough Q&A.

7) Track message penetration, not just mentions: coverage without key messages is wasted opportunity.
