# Treasury Management Overview

## Introduction
Treasury management encompasses cash flow management, liquidity optimization, investment decisions, and financial risk mitigation to ensure organizational financial health and maximize returns on assets.

## Core Treasury Functions

### 1. Cash Management
Daily cash operations, working capital optimization, and cash flow forecasting.
→ **See**: [cash-management.md](cash-management.md)

### 2. Liquidity Planning
Strategic liquidity management, stress testing, and liquidity buffer optimization.
→ **See**: [liquidity-planning.md](liquidity-planning.md)

### 3. Investment Management
Short-term investments, portfolio construction, and return optimization.
→ **See**: [investment-management-treasury.md](investment-management-treasury.md)

### 4. Risk Management
Interest rate risk, foreign exchange risk, and counterparty risk mitigation.
→ **See**: [risk-management-treasury.md](risk-management-treasury.md)

### 5. Treasury Operations
Technology infrastructure, banking relationships, and operational excellence.
→ **See**: [treasury-operations.md](treasury-operations.md)

## Key Objectives
- Ensure adequate liquidity for operations
- Optimize cash positioning and returns
- Manage financial risks effectively
- Maintain banking relationships
- Ensure regulatory compliance
- Support strategic initiatives

## Best Practices
1. Forecast cash flows accurately
2. Maintain adequate liquidity buffers
3. Diversify investments and counterparties
4. Hedge material financial risks
5. Automate treasury processes
6. Monitor performance continuously
7. Ensure strong governance and controls
8. Stay compliant with regulations
